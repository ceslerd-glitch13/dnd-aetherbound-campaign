# Ironforge City Profile

**Status:** Primary Dwarven Dimensional Gate City  
**Population:** Approximately 42,000  
**Racial Breakdown:** Dwarf ~75%, Human ~15%, Gnome ~7%, Other ~3%  
**Primary Economy:** Mining, heavy industry, weapons, armor, engineered magic, and dimensional trade  
**Government:** Clan Council led by a High Thane

## City Identity

Ironforge is Elyndria's dwarven gate city and strongest center of heavy engineering, fortification, military manufacturing, and integrated magic.

Dwarven magical practice begins with a mechanically sound weapon, tool, structure, or machine. Runes and enchantments improve accuracy, durability, impact, efficiency, control, or defensive strength rather than replacing the underlying design. When anti-magic suppresses an enhancement, the physical object remains useful.

## Leadership

- **High Thane Thrain Ironfist** — Stern, fair leader of the Clan Council who balances gate defense with industrial needs.
- **Master Engineer Garrick Stonehammer** — Brilliant and impatient overseer of gate engineering, mines, and major infrastructure.
- **Forge Mistress Helga Emberforge** — Demanding, honorable leader of major forge production and integrated magical armament.
- **Captain Drogath Stonefist** — Suspicious and disciplined commander of the Deep Guard and physical defense of the Gate Heart.
- **Trade Envoy Varric Stonecoin** — Courteous, calculating manager of foreign trade and supply relationships.
- **Lorekeeper Brannok Runebeard** — Stubborn, dry-witted guardian of ancient records, designs, and warnings.

## Districts

### The Gate Heart

A massive underground fortress containing the dimensional gate, inspection chambers, anti-magic suppression areas, artillery positions, layered doors, physical barriers, quarantine rooms, and command stations.

### The Forges

The industrial center of the city, producing tools, armor, weapons, runes, machinery, structural components, and gate-defense equipment.

### The Deep Mines

Extensive mining tunnels, survey halls, freight stations, ventilation systems, and controlled passages beneath and around the city.

### Clan Halls

Residential, political, religious, and professional districts organized around clans, guilds, apprenticeships, and communal institutions.

### Trade Caverns

Markets, banks, gem exchanges, dimensional-goods warehouses, freight terminals, and contract offices.

## Key Locations and Proprietors

### Inns and Taverns

- **The Anvil's Rest — Marta Coalbraid:** Loud inn popular with soldiers, miners, and contract crews. Marta is generous, blunt, and well informed about missing workers.
- **The Deep Hearth — Oskar Deepmug:** Quiet tavern favored by engineers and scholars. Oskar hears technical problems before they reach official reports.

### Shops and Services

- **The Great Forge Public Works — Dagna Flintmantle:** Public commissions and masterwork orders under Helga Emberforge's authority. Dagna is efficient and exact about deadlines.
- **Stonehammer Engineering — Berrin Gearlock:** Public workshop associated with Garrick's engineering guild. Berrin builds prototypes intended to function during anti-magic suppression.
- **Rune & Relic — Odrun Relicward:** Licensed relics, integrated magic, and artifact examination. Odrun is cautious and protective of dangerous objects.
- **Ironvein Traders — Tilda Coppermark:** Historic trading house with no family connection to Ravensport's Vorn Ironvein. Tilda is disciplined and unforgiving of false accounting.
- **Deepward Armory — Hargin Shieldthane:** Approved military and defensive equipment. Hargin recognizes stolen Deep Guard gear and restricted maker's marks.
- **Gemcutter's Hall — Runa Gemheart:** Fine gems, jewelry, rune settings, and precision crystal work. Runa can examine unfamiliar crystal structures without activating them.

## Basic Services

- **Lodging and meals:** Clan guest halls, miners' inns, military lodges, merchant housing, and private rooms are widespread. Clan or trade connections improve access and price.
- **Mundane equipment and repairs:** Exceptional weapons, armor, tools, mining equipment, locks, wagons, machinery, and replacement parts are produced locally. Common repairs are fast; experimental or military work requires a respected workshop.
- **Technological services:** Mechanical lifts, rail systems, industrial machinery, siege engines, ventilation, inspection devices, and physical gate defenses are highly advanced. Military plans and proprietary clan designs are restricted.
- **Healing and medical care:** Physicians, surgeons, alchemists, prosthetic specialists, temple healers, and magical practitioners are available. Gate and mine hospitals remain functional during anti-magic activation.
- **Temples and spiritual assistance:** Clan shrines, ancestor halls, forge temples, and sanctuaries provide rites, counsel, healing, and funerals. Sacred crafting and duty are central themes.
- **Transportation and stabling:** Freight lifts, underground rail carts, wagons, pack animals, mountain roads, and gate transport are available. Heavy cargo movement is especially efficient.
- **Banking and secure storage:** Clan vaults, gem exchanges, trade banks, bonded warehouses, and physical storage are exceptionally secure. Premium access depends on reputation or sponsorship.
- **Messengers and information:** Runners, bells, speaking tubes, mechanical message systems, guild couriers, and magical communication operate in parallel.
- **Legal help and permits:** Clan advocates, contract scribes, trade officials, mining registrars, engineering inspectors, and gate authorities manage disputes and licenses.
- **Hiring labor and guards:** Miners, smiths, engineers, mechanics, caravan guards, guides, excavation crews, and mercenaries are easy to find. Skilled workers may refuse work that violates contracts or professional honor.
- **Magical services:** Rune work, enchantment, identification, magical repair, weapon enhancement, armor reinforcement, wards, ammunition enhancement, powered tools, and artifact study are highly developed.

### Integrated Dwarven Magic

Common Ironforge applications include:

- rune-enhanced blades and guided ammunition;
- reinforced armor and warded shields;
- impact-amplifying hammers;
- heat-controlled forges;
- targeting and range-finding systems;
- powered excavation and lifting tools;
- defensive mechanisms combining physical engineering with enchantment.

Enchantments enhance strong physical designs. They do not replace them.

## DM Scene Anchors

These working locations support prepared hooks without fixing exact tunnels, levels, or encounter maps.

- **Copperglass Workshop:** An independent workshop on the boundary between the Trade Caverns and the Forges, beneath civilian housing. Its copper-framed front glass is cracked from an internal accident. This is the primary location for **The Unclaimed Workshop**.
- **Ash Rail Spur:** A disused freight tunnel behind Copperglass Workshop that provides a concealed loading route and evidence of the missing engineer's removal.
- **Foundry Receiving Hall Three:** A loud weighing and distribution floor near the Great Forge Public Works. It anchors the **Missing Alloy** hook and its contradictory delivery records.
- **Emberforge Pattern Vault:** A secured archive beneath a restricted forge office containing physical templates, rune layouts, and controlled manufacturing instructions. It anchors the **Escaped Design** hook.
- **Deep Guard Drill Gallery:** A reinforced training corridor near, but outside, the most restricted Gate Heart areas. It anchors the **Defense Knowledge** hook and shows where physical and magical defenses are tested together.

## Trade and Supply

### Major Exports

- Weapons, armor, shields, ammunition, tools, mining equipment, and heavy machinery.
- Siege engines, defensive mechanisms, gate systems, locks, vaults, and industrial infrastructure.
- Refined metals, specialty alloys, cut gems, rune-enhanced equipment, and integrated magical machinery.
- Engineering expertise, fortification design, military contracts, and large-scale repair.

### Major Imports

- Grain, produce, livestock, timber, cloth, leather, medicine, herbs, oils, and alchemical reagents.
- Fine magical materials, living components, rare woods, and enchantment supplies from Faerindel.
- Dimensional ores, experimental technology, luxury goods, and specialist knowledge.

### Restricted Goods

- Military-grade weapons, siege plans, prototype machinery, gate-defense systems, and clan-owned designs.
- High explosives, restricted runes, enchanted ammunition, armor-piercing devices, and unregistered dimensional metals.
- Sacred relics, sealed records, and components tied to the Gate Heart.

### Black Market

Serious contraband is hidden behind clan, guild, military, or contracting relationships. Common illicit goods include stolen weapons, counterfeit maker's marks, proprietary designs, restricted runes, explosives, gate salvage, and relics.

Street criminals handle minor theft; dangerous contraband usually requires workshop, clan, military, or warehouse access. Penalties are severe because stolen designs threaten both trade dominance and city defense.

### Current Supply Pressure

Food, timber, medicine, and other surface necessities remain vulnerable to mountain-route disruption. Military production also competes with civilian demand for metal and skilled labor.

## Daily Life and Current Tensions

Work, clan obligations, military readiness, engineering, trade, and gate defense are woven into ordinary life. Residents accept drills, freight redirection, sealed passages, and emergency anti-magic activation as routine civic responsibilities.

Current tensions center on:

- military versus civilian production;
- clan control of contracts and apprenticeships;
- proprietary designs and foreign technical access;
- skilled-labor shortages;
- food and timber supply vulnerability;
- responsibility for unexplained mechanical failures;
- whether recent attacks reveal knowledge of restricted defensive systems.

Dwarven factions argue over doctrine, resources, and authority—not over the need to protect the gate.

## Gate, Dead-Zone, and Cultural Interaction

- Access to the Gate Heart is based on authorization and risk, not ancestry.
- Human logisticians, elven magical specialists, and gnome mechanics are established parts of the city.
- Ironforge's institutional strength is physical defense, engineering, weapons, redundancy, and integrated magical equipment.
- Faerindel's broad spellcraft and living systems complement rather than oppose Ironforge methods.
- Deepanvil is a critical partner because its equipment and expertise remain dependable when active magic fails.

---

## Defenses

- Multiple layers of thick stone fortifications and controlled underground approaches.
- Elite Deep Guard under Drogath Stonefist.
- Heavy doors, mechanical traps, physical artillery, armored troops, alarms, and fallback corridors.
- Permanent inspection areas and emergency anti-magic fields inside the Gate Heart.
- Rune-enhanced systems backed by physical mechanisms that continue operating when enchantments are suppressed.
- Regular patrols through mines and surrounding mountain routes.
- Strong cooperative defense arrangements with Ravensport and Faerindel.

## Immediate Plot Hooks

1. **The Unclaimed Workshop:** A missing engineer left behind a prototype connecting mundane machinery to an unfamiliar blue-crystal interface.
2. **Missing Alloy:** A shipment recorded as delivered never reached the public forge floor.
3. **Escaped Design:** Restricted enchantment methods have appeared outside a secure workshop.
4. **Civilian Shortage:** Military production is consuming materials and labor needed for mine and public infrastructure repairs.
5. **Defense Knowledge:** An unknown enemy appears to understand how Ironforge's physical and magical systems overlap.
