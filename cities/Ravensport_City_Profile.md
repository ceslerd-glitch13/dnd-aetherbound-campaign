# Ravensport City Profile

**Status:** Major Trade Hub and Human-Led Dimensional Gate City  
**Population:** Approximately 48,000 permanent residents plus a large transient population  
**Racial Breakdown:** Human ~55%, Elf ~20%, Dwarf ~15%, Other ~10%  
**Primary Economy:** Dimensional trade, harbor trade, warehousing, finance, and regional distribution  
**Government:** Lord Protector with a mixed City Council

## City Identity

Ravensport is Elyndria's largest eastern port and most diverse gate city. Almost anything can be found here, but valuable, magical, foreign, or dimensional goods attract permits, tariffs, inspections, and official attention.

The city has operated around its dimensional gate for centuries. Residents regard distant alarms, military movement toward the Heart, and the sounds of contained incursions as serious but ordinary facts of life.

## Leadership

### Civil Government and City Council

- **Lord Protector Elara Voss** — Current civilian ruler and council chair. Calm, compassionate, and administratively relentless; she controls civil administration, diplomacy, taxation, appointments, and emergency civilian authority.
- **Harlan Voss** — Retired former Lord Protector and Elara's father. Blunt and strategic; he advises privately but has no regular vote or operational command.
- **Gate Warden Seraphine Vale** — Voting council member responsible for gate-security policy, defensive readiness, Iron Talon doctrine, and coordination between military branches.
- **Master of Trade Vorn Ironvein** — Voting council member representing guilds, tariffs, dimensional commerce, logistics, and major commercial interests.
- **Archmage Lirael Starveil** — Voting council member leading the Arcane Wardens and advising on magical threats, Aetherite technology, and dimensional research.
- **Captain Thorne Blackwood** — Voting council member and commander of ordinary city law enforcement, investigations, and district patrols outside the Heart.

### Operational Gate Command

- **Gate Commander Kael Thorne** — Operational commander of the Heart and the Iron Talons. He reports to Seraphine Vale and holds tactical authority during active gate emergencies.
- Kael attends relevant security sessions but is not a permanent voting council member.
- Kael Thorne and Thorne Blackwood are unrelated.

## Districts

### Docks District

A crowded river and coastal port centered on exactly four working piers: **North Pier, East Pier, South Pier, and Merchants' Pier**. Sailors, freight crews, shipwrights, taverns, warehouses, and customs brokers dominate the district.

### Grand Bazaar

The commercial heart of Ravensport, containing permanent shops, rotating stalls, banks, money changers, advocates, translators, and merchant offices.

### Gate Quarter — The Heart

The heavily fortified inner district containing the dimensional gate. Entry requires identification or sponsorship, a legitimate purpose, inspection of people and cargo, declaration of restricted magic or technology, and compliance with anti-magic screening.

Heavy gate-level security is concentrated here. Ordinary citizens moving through the rest of Ravensport are not subjected to Heart inspections.

### Shrouded Wards

A poorer district of workshops, crowded housing, private warehouses, pawnshops, and concealed criminal businesses. Ravensport's largest black-market network operates here.

### Residential District

The city's broadest district, ranging from working-class housing to comfortable merchant homes. Mixed-race neighborhoods are normal and often several generations old.

### Noble Hill

A smaller, elevated district containing noble estates, wealthy merchant residences, private gardens, guarded compounds, and diplomatic lodging.

## Key Locations and Proprietors

### Inns and Taverns

- **The Rusty Anchor — Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik:** A lively riverfront inn popular with sailors and adventurers. Rurik is a grizzled, blunt but fair semi-retired captain whose stolen cargo launches the starter quest.
- **The Gilded Gate — Selene Brightwater:** An upscale inn for wealthy traders and officials. Selene is gracious, discreet, and exceptionally good at remembering private negotiations.

### Shops and Services

- **Arcane Emporium — Avenna Quillstar:** Licensed spell components, scrolls, identification, and minor enchantment. Avenna is precise and legally required to report dangerous undeclared artifacts.
- **Iron & Thread — Dorrin Stonehand:** Armor, clothing, uniforms, and protective travel gear. Dorrin is a cheerful perfectionist who notices counterfeit work.
- **Blade & Bolt — Torren Ironclad:** Weapons, crossbows, repairs, and licensed defensive gear. Torren can identify workshops by balance and tool marks.
- **Essence & Elixir — Mirael Thornwood:** Medicine, antidotes, alchemical goods, and mundane treatment. Mirael is kind, practical, and suspicious of unusual dockside illnesses.
- **Last Chance Curios — Corvin Rook:** Pawnshop and low-level fencing operation. Corvin knows Shrouded Wards routes but fears Nyra Vell's network.
- **The Caravan Outfitter — Mara Reed:** Wagons, animals, route maps, guides, and expedition supplies. Mara knows which western caravan losses are ordinary and which are not.

### Recurring Contacts

- **Maelis “Silverthorn”:** Half-elven server and information broker at the Rusty Anchor. A distant Voss relative who quietly gathers intelligence and recognizes an unmarked Aetherite power orb when she sees one.
- **Silas Crowe:** Polished intermediary serving Veyne's organization. He commissioned Rurik's delivery and evaluates useful adventurers before treating them as threats.
- **Nyra “the Thread” Vell:** Pragmatic leader of a major Shrouded Wards smuggling network. She trades in routes, documents, and information more than loyalty.
- **Brogan Ashdelve:** Dwarven caravan liaison coordinating supplies between Ravensport and Deepanvil.

## Basic Services

- **Lodging and meals:** Available at every price level. Cheap lodging clusters around the docks and Shrouded Wards; comfortable merchant inns and expensive Noble Hill lodging are common. Prices rise during major gate arrivals, festivals, and shipping delays.
- **Mundane equipment and repairs:** Common weapons, armor, adventuring supplies, ship equipment, clothing, and tools are readily available. Military equipment, siege gear, gate-security devices, and official uniforms require authorization.
- **Healing and medical care:** Physicians, apothecaries, temple healers, and magical healing are available. Restoration, curse removal, resurrection, and treatment of unusual planar conditions require wealth, sponsorship, or senior approval.
- **Temples and spiritual assistance:** Numerous recognized faiths maintain temples, shrines, and chapels. Dangerous rituals, resurrection, divine investigations, and ceremonies involving unknown planar beings receive scrutiny.
- **Transportation and stabling:** Stables, wagon yards, barges, ships, couriers, and carriages are common. Actual gate travel requires documents, inspection, declared cargo, tariffs, and authorization.
- **Banking and secure storage:** Merchant banks, guild vaults, money changers, letters of credit, and warehouses are widespread. Large deposits, dimensional currency, artifacts, and Aetherite technology may be inspected or reported.
- **Messengers and information:** Runners, guild couriers, ship messengers, notices, tavern rumors, and merchant networks are easy to find. Magical communication is expensive and may be monitored when gate security is involved.
- **Legal help and permits:** Advocates, scribes, translators, customs offices, and permit offices handle licenses, cargo declarations, gate documents, weapon permits, and commercial claims.
- **Hiring labor and guards:** Sailors, dockworkers, caravan hands, translators, guides, licensed guards, mercenaries, and adventurers can be hired openly. Active city guards and Iron Talons cannot be hired privately.
- **Magical services:** Components, scrolls, identification, minor enchantment, magical repair, and common magic are available through licensed practitioners. Teleportation, planar travel, powerful divination, summoning, and experimentation near the Heart are restricted.

## DM Scene Anchors

These working locations give the DM a recognizable starting point without fixing exact streets, floorplans, travel times, or encounter layouts.

### Starter Investigation

- **The Wayward Gull:** Rurik's working ship, normally moored at South Pier near the Rusty Anchor. Its rear-deck strongroom is the starter quest's initial evidence scene; a narrow loading ramp, tired deckhands, and a newly replaced lock make it recognizable.
- **Netmaker's Bunkhouse:** A crowded temporary-worker boarding house on the boundary between the Docks District and the Shrouded Wards. Orren Pike's rented room, evidence of his debts, the outdated dock badge, and witnesses to his gray-scarved contact can be found here.
- **Old Gull Sailworks:** An abandoned canal-side sailmaker's building in the Shrouded Wards, roughly inland from East Pier. A faded white gull marks the doors, hanging canvas conceals the upper loft, and a canal loading entrance provides alternate access. This is the medallion recovery location.

### Open-World Investigations

- **The Lantern House:** An old lamp-oil warehouse beside a narrow Shrouded Wards canal. A blue-shaded lantern marks the side entrance on auction nights. This is the location of **The Blue Lantern Auction**.
- **The Harbor Watchhouse:** A guard station between the Docks District and the secured approach to the Heart. It contains patrol offices, holding cells, evidence storage, and a public reporting desk, and serves as Captain Thorne Blackwood's practical investigative base.
- **The West Response Annex:** A fortified records and equipment building just outside the Heart's primary civilian checkpoint. It holds response logs, recovered physical markers, damaged gear, and processed creature remains for **The Third Alarm**.
- **North Intake Yard, Quarantine Passage Four, and Riverward Containment Court:** Three named Heart incident sites used in **The Third Alarm**. Their exact internal placement and layouts remain flexible.
- **Customs Warehouse Seven:** A city-controlled holding warehouse directly behind Merchants' Pier for delayed, disputed, or suspicious cargo. It anchors the **Cargo Backlog** hook and its falsified clearance records.
- **Red Wheel Freight Yard:** A freight-transfer yard on the inland edge of the Grand Bazaar, recognized by a large red wagon wheel over its gate. It anchors western-shipment records, after-hours transfers, and the **Western Transfers** hook.
- **Deepanvil Freight Office:** A small stone-fronted office inside Red Wheel Freight Yard used by Brogan Ashdelve and other Deepanvil caravan representatives. It provides supply schedules, missing manifests, western-road contacts, and access to **The Vanished Supply Caravan**.

## Trade and Supply

### Major Exports and Services

- Re-exported dimensional and continental goods distributed through the harbor, river, roads, and gate network.
- Fish, preserved seafood, ship supplies, rope, sailcloth, and processed dockside goods.
- Warehousing, brokerage, customs services, banking, letters of credit, and caravan coordination.
- Finished consumer goods assembled from imported materials.
- Translation, transportation, information, and gate-navigation services.

### Major Imports

- Food from surrounding farms and inland routes.
- Ore, metalwork, machinery, weapons, and tools from dwarven cities.
- Enchanted goods, medicine, herbs, textiles, and magical services from Faerindel.
- Timber, stone, livestock, fuel, luxury goods, and dimensional cargo.

### Restricted Goods

- Aetherite crystals and technology.
- Unregistered dimensional artifacts or living specimens.
- Military weapons, siege equipment, gate-security devices, and official uniforms.
- Dangerous components, summoning materials, cursed objects, and undeclared planar cargo.
- Forged travel documents, permits, customs seals, and restricted maps.

### Black Market

Ravensport's black market is large, organized, and active. It is concentrated in the Shrouded Wards, docks, private warehouses, corrupt brokerage offices, and false-front shipping companies. Common illicit trade includes stolen cargo, forged papers, undeclared dimensional goods, restricted magic, Aetherite-related objects, and bribed inspections.

Authorities suppress visible crime but cannot eliminate smuggling without disrupting legitimate trade.

### Current Supply Pressure

Security-related cargo backlogs have increased warehouse fees and created opportunities for bribery, theft, false documentation, and preferential clearance.

## Daily Life and Current Tensions

Ravensport's ordinary districts remain busy, commercial, cosmopolitan, and comparatively free of gate-level intrusion. Residents are accustomed to distant alarms, military movement toward the Heart, temporary road closures, and the sounds of contained incursions.

Current tensions center on:

- cargo delays and warehouse costs;
- unequal access to contracts and priority clearance;
- dock wages and labor conditions;
- corruption, favoritism, and forged permits;
- how much authority officials should exercise over merchants and visiting specialists;
- whether recent incursions show unusual coordination or intelligence.

Ordinary criminals may smuggle goods, bribe officials, or falsify records, but intentional weakening of the gate is regarded as an existential crime rather than routine criminal activity.

## Gate, Dead-Zone, and Cultural Interaction

- Heart access is based on purpose, documentation, cargo, and security risk—not ancestry.
- Human, elven, and dwarven residents have lived together for generations.
- Ravensport specializes in logistics, administration, adaptable staffing, diplomacy, and coordinating mixed institutions.
- Elven magical specialists and dwarven engineers are permanent parts of gate defense and city commerce.
- Expeditions toward nearby dead zones require nonmagical communication, medical backups, and equipment that remains useful without active magic.

---

## Defenses

- Massive fortified walls and controlled access around the Heart.
- Eight watchtowers along the inner defensive walls.
- Approximately **1,800 active military personnel**: roughly 1,000 assigned to the Heart and approximately 800 to city-wide guard duties.
- **200 Iron Talons**, organized into four companies of 50 under Kael Thorne's operational command and Seraphine Vale's strategic oversight.
- **80 Arcane Wardens** under Lirael Starveil.
- Standard city guards under Thorne Blackwood.
- Strong physical fortifications, anti-teleportation wards, alarms, mechanical backups, and dimensional containment.
- Permanent controlled anti-magic inspection areas and larger emergency suppression zones inside the Heart.
- Physical barriers, runners, signals, conventional medicine, and mundane weapons remain operational when magic is suppressed.

## Immediate Plot Hooks

1. **Captain Rurik's Request:** Recover the stolen Resonance Medallion and the unmarked Aetherite orb concealed inside it.
2. **The Blue Lantern Auction:** A private Shrouded Wards auction includes damaged Aetherite technology sought by a concealed buyer.
3. **The Third Alarm:** Repeated gate incursions appear to be measuring Heart response times.
4. **Cargo Backlog:** Someone is using legitimate security delays to hide theft and preferential treatment.
5. **Western Transfers:** Mining equipment and unusual components are being redirected toward an unknown installation west of the city.
