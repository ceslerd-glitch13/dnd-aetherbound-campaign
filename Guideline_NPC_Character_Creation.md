# Guideline: NPC & Character Creation (Key Locations)

**Purpose**: This document explains how to create memorable and useful NPCs for important locations in your campaign. Focus on **key locations** only — not every single shop or house needs a named character.

---

## 1. When to Create a Named NPC
Create a notable NPC when:
- The location is **recurring** or important to the story.
- Players are likely to **return** to the location.
- The NPC can provide **information, quests, goods, or roleplaying opportunities**.
- The NPC serves a **plot or relationship purpose**.

**Rule of Thumb**: If players are only going to visit once and it’s not plot-relevant, you can improvise or leave it generic.

## 2. Types of Key Locations That Usually Need NPCs
- Taverns / Inns (at least the owner or bartender)
- Major Shops (magic shop, weapon shop, general store)
- Temples / Religious Centers
- Government Buildings (town hall, lord’s keep)
- Guard / Military Headquarters
- Guild Halls
- Fences / Pawn Shops (especially shady ones)
- Libraries or Scholarly Centers
- Any location tied to a **quest** or **secret**

## 3. The 3-Line NPC Method (Fast & Effective)
For quick but memorable NPCs, use this simple structure:

**Line 1 – Appearance**  
What the players see when they first meet the NPC (looks, clothing, what they’re doing).

**Line 2 – Personality + Role**  
What the NPC does for a living and their general personality or attitude.

**Line 3 – Story Hook**  
How this NPC can help (or hinder) the players or advance the plot. What do they want? What do they know?

**Example**:
- **Appearance**: Gruff dwarven woman with a soot-stained apron and a missing finger.
- **Personality + Role**: Runs the local forge. Blunt, no-nonsense, but fair.
- **Story Hook**: Knows about a recent shipment of strange blackpowder that went missing.

## 4. Deeper NPC Creation (When Needed)
For more important or recurring NPCs, consider adding:

- **Name** (first + last or title)
- **Race / Age / Appearance**
- **Personality Traits** (at least 2–3)
- **Motivation / Goal** (what do they want?)
- **Secret** (something they’re hiding)
- **Quirk** (a memorable habit or speech pattern)
- **Connections** (who do they know? Allies or enemies?)
- **Voice / Mannerisms** (how do they talk and act?)

## 5. Front Desk / Gatekeeper NPCs
Not every player needs direct access to the "head honcho." Sometimes it’s more realistic to have:
- A receptionist, assistant, or guard who controls access.
- A lower-ranking employee who knows some information but not everything.
- A suspicious or loyal subordinate who must be convinced or bypassed.

This adds realism and creates roleplaying opportunities.

## 6. Best Practices
- **Don’t over-prep** minor NPCs. A name + one strong trait is often enough.
- Give recurring NPCs **evolving relationships** with the party.
- Make sure every notable NPC has a **purpose** (information, goods, quest hook, emotional connection, or obstacle).
- Use the **"What do they want?"** question as your north star.
- Keep a simple list or note for recurring NPCs so you stay consistent.

## 7. Quick NPC Creation Questions
Ask yourself:
1. Why is this NPC here?
2. What do they want from the players (or what do the players want from them)?
3. What makes them memorable?
4. Do they know something useful?
5. Can they create conflict or tension?

---

**Final Tip**:  
Focus on **quality over quantity**. A handful of well-developed, recurring NPCs will make your world feel alive far more than dozens of throwaway characters.

Use this guideline whenever you create a new key location.
