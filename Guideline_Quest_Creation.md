# Guideline: Quest Creation

**Purpose**: This document provides a reusable framework for creating quests in a fantasy RPG campaign. It focuses on structure, discovery, and consistency without relying on campaign-specific terms.

---

## 1. Quest Types
- **Main / Story Quests**: Advance the primary plot.
- **Side Quests**: Optional content that adds depth or rewards.
- **Discovery Quests**: Found through exploration, conversation, or investigation (not posted publicly).
- **Recurring Quests**: Can be repeated or have ongoing elements.
- **Escalating Quests**: Start small and grow in scope over time.

## 2. Discovery Methods
Quests can be found through:
- Posted notices or job boards
- Conversations with NPCs
- Overhearing rumors in taverns or public spaces
- Finding notes, journals, or clues
- Exploring locations
- Being approached by an NPC in need

## 3. Basic Quest Structure
A good quest generally includes:
1. **Hook** — Why the players get involved.
2. **Investigation / Travel** — Gathering information or reaching a location.
3. **Conflict / Challenge** — The main obstacle (combat, puzzle, social, etc.).
4. **Resolution** — How the situation is resolved.
5. **Reward / Consequence** — What the players gain or what changes in the world.

## 4. Key Elements Every Quest Should Have
- Clear goal or objective
- Stakes (what happens if they succeed or fail)
- Complications or twists (to keep it interesting)
- Reward (gold, items, information, reputation, story progression)
- Connection to the world (ties into locations, factions, or ongoing events)

## 5. Scaling Quests
- Adjust difficulty based on party level and size.
- Use appropriate enemies or challenges for the expected power level.
- Offer multiple paths to success when possible (combat, stealth, diplomacy, clever planning).

## 6. Best Practices
- Quests should feel like they belong in the world.
- Give players agency and meaningful choices.
- Balance combat with roleplaying and exploration opportunities.
- Consider long-term consequences of quest outcomes.
- Keep quest descriptions clear but allow room for player creativity.

---

Use this as a template when creating new quests to maintain consistency across the campaign.
