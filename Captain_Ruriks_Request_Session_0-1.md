**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Player Level:** 1–3 (ideal starting level)  
**Location:** Ravensport (Docks District)

## Session 0 Goals
- Character introductions and backstory ties

- Give players their first job and a useful magic item
- Hook them toward the larger plot involving Veyne's organization and Aetheron

## The Hook – Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik

**Where the players meet him**: The Rusty Anchor tavern and inn in the Docks District. It is a lively, slightly rowdy place popular with sailors, adventurers, and merchants.

Rurik is a veteran independent ship captain and tavern proprietor. He is semi-retired from routine sailing but still owns his ship and accepts selective, profitable, or sensitive cargo contracts. He is not related to the Voss family.

**Full Description of The Rusty Anchor** (for DM reference):  
The Rusty Anchor is a large, two-story wooden building right on the riverfront. A large, slightly crooked anchor hangs above the main door. Inside, the common room is always busy. The air smells of salt, ale, roasted meat, and pipe smoke. It’s loud, warm, and lively.

**Staff**:
- **Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik** (Owner) – Grizzled former sailor with a black eyepatch.
- **Mira “Red” Tanner** (Head Bartender & Cook) – Tough, no-nonsense woman with bright red hair.
- **Jorik Stonehand** (Bouncer & Handyman) – Broad dwarf who breaks up fights.
- **Lirael "Silverthorn"** (Waitress & Information Broker, Half-elf) – Friendly and chatty. She is a distant relative of the ruling Voss family but hides this connection. She uses the name "Silverthorn" publicly and gathers intelligence for her family while enjoying the dockside chaos. One of the best sources of rumors in the Docks.
- Young staff (busboys, etc.).

**Menu**: Rusty Anchor Ale, grilled river fish, salted pork stew, sailor’s platter, spiced rum drinks.

---

**Rurik’s Pitch** (read or paraphrase):

> “Three days ago I was hired to bring a small, unassuming shipment from one of the outer ports straight to Ravensport. Nothing flashy — just an ordinary-looking silver necklace in a locked box. The client paid half upfront and promised the rest on delivery.  
> 
> Last night, right after we docked, the box was stolen from my strongroom. No signs of forced entry. I’m certain it was an inside job or someone who knew exactly what they were after.  
> 
> The problem is… the final client is **very** important and very impatient. If I don’t recover that necklace, I’m not getting the rest of my payment — and I might lose my ship.  
> 
> Find who took it, get the necklace back, and there’s a fat reward in it for you. I don’t care how you do it. Just bring me that damn necklace.”

**Reward**:
- 200 gp total (50 gp upfront, 150 gp on return)
- Free room & board at The Rusty Anchor for a month
- Future favors from Captain Rurik


**DM Note — Client Identity:** The final client is **Silas Crowe**, an agent of Lord Cassian Veyne. Rurik knows Silas only as a wealthy and dangerous intermediary; Rurik does not know the orb's role in Veyne's travel array.

## The Resonance Medallion (The Stolen Item)

### What It Is

- The ordinary silver medallion is only a concealed protective housing.
- Inside it is a smooth, unmarked Aetherite crystal orb with no preset spell.
- The orb is the valuable technology; it can be removed from the necklace and used in other devices.
- No attunement is required.

### Charge and Recharge

- Maximum capacity: **24 charges**.
- Recharge: **1 charge per hour** while exposed to usable ambient magic.
- A fully depleted orb recharges in 24 hours.
- It does not recharge inside a normal dead zone unless another approved resonance source provides usable magic.

### Dead-Zone Protection

- The orb creates a **5-foot-radius resonance field centered on itself**.
- The field protects the holder and any creature directly adjacent to the orb.
- Maintaining the field inside a dead zone costs **1 charge per hour for the field as a whole**, not per creature.
- Protected creatures do not suffer the dead zone's direct magical drain or accumulate Magical Exhaustion while charges remain.
- When the orb reaches 0 charges, normal dead-zone effects begin immediately. Protected time does not create retroactive exhaustion.

### Spellcasting Through the Orb

- A protected caster may use the orb's stored energy to cast a spell inside a dead zone.
- Spell cost is **8 charges per spell level**:
  - 1st level: 8 charges
  - 2nd level: 16 charges
  - 3rd level: 24 charges
- Cantrips consume no charges and cannot be upcast.
- The caster must still know or prepare the spell and normally be able to cast spells of that level.
- The orb does not independently support spells above 3rd level.
- Instantaneous effects resolve normally.
- Ongoing, concentration, or persistent effects remain stable only while the relevant caster, target, or effect remains within the orb's field.
- The orb can support one magical-transport endpoint inside its field, but transport still fails if another required endpoint lies in an unprotected dead zone.
- The orb does not automatically override a deliberate anti-magic field.

### Hidden Campaign Significance — DM Only

- Silas Crowe, acting for Lord Cassian Veyne, commissioned the delivery.
- Veyne's Aetheron travel array requires exactly three fully charged Aetherite crystals: one rune-inscribed transit crystal and two unmarked battery orbs.
- The stolen orb was intended to serve as one of those two batteries.
- The full array provides 72 charges and consumes 56 charges for one successful Plane Shift-equivalent trip to Aetheron.
