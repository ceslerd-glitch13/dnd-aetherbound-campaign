# Shops, Stores, and Item Availability

## Purpose

This is the central operational reference for named shop inventories, general settlement equipment availability, price guidance, permits, restricted goods, black markets, and dead-zone purchasing rules across Elyndria.

City profiles remain authoritative for each location's identity, proprietor, trade role, services, restrictions, and black-market character. This file determines what equipment can reasonably be purchased or ordered and under what conditions.

## Approved Source Baseline

- **Equipment source snapshot:** D&D Beyond equipment catalog reviewed 2026-07-17.
- **Rules foundation:** D&D 5th Edition, using current core item names and listed prices where practical.
- **Legacy gaps:** Familiar 2014 core listings are retained when the reviewed catalog omits campaign-useful essentials. Every such entry is marked **2014 legacy**.
- **Excluded by default:** Partner content, setting-specific third-party gear, and the separate magic-item catalog.
- **Price stability:** The prices recorded here are the approved 2026-07-17 campaign snapshot. Later changes to D&D Beyond do not automatically alter campaign prices.
- **Aetherite:** Aetherite, resonance technology, gate-security devices, and Aetheronian equipment never receive automatic retail prices.

## Approved Availability Decisions

1. Current core equipment plus clearly marked 2014 legacy gaps is the campaign's normal equipment-price baseline.
2. Ordinary pistols and muskets exist but are licensed specialist goods rather than universal stock.
3. Revolvers, shotguns, hunting rifles, semiautomatic weapons, automatic weapons, energy weapons, and antimatter weapons are not normal Elyndrian commerce. They remain restricted dimensional finds or story-only equipment until separately approved.
4. Airships are story-only and are not ordinary Elyndrian transport.
5. Deepanvil and Ironforge have excellent medical and alchemical services, but no additional named medical storefront or proprietor is created by this reference.
6. Black-market entries describe possible access through the correct network. They are not guaranteed shelves, and serious purchases create witnesses, debts, investigations, favors, or other consequences.

## Coverage

This reference provides guidance for:

- all **35 canonical cities** plus **Westlake Port**;
- all **24 named shops, markets, workshops, and public commercial services** in the four profiled cities;
- all **8 named inns and taverns**;
- the distinct black markets of Ravensport, Deepanvil, Faerindel, and Ironforge;
- named and unnamed apothecary, herbalist, alchemical, surgical, and medical services;
- firearms, poisons, explosives, vehicles, restricted technology, Aetherite, and dead-zone operation.

## Availability Labels

- **Common:** Usually on the shelf; ordinary quantities do not require a check.
- **Specialist:** Available in the settlement through an appropriate craft or trade seller.
- **Order:** Not normally stocked; a deposit and waiting period are expected.
- **Permit:** Legal only with identity, purpose, paperwork, sponsorship, or a registered institution.
- **Restricted:** Not ordinary retail; government, military, clan, academy, or Gate Heart approval is required.
- **Black market:** Illegal or knowingly diverted goods. Access creates risk, leverage, and witnesses.
- **Story only:** Not assigned to retail. Introduction requires a deliberate campaign decision.

## Price Guidance

Use the listed price as the normal base price. Apply these modifiers only when useful:

| Situation | Suggested adjustment |
|---|---:|
| Locally produced common good | Listed price or 10% lower |
| Imported but routinely supplied | Listed price to 25% higher |
| Current shortage | 25%–50% higher |
| Special order | 25% deposit; final price 10%–25% higher |
| Black-market restricted good | 50%–200% higher, plus a favor or complication |

Quantities remain flexible. A listed item means the location can reasonably sell, order, broker, or refer the party to it; it does not mean unlimited stock is always present.

## Settlement Equipment Availability

This document assigns a practical equipment range to all 35 canonical cities and Westlake Port. It does not create shopkeepers or storefront names for settlements that lack profiles.

Use the template codes in each settlement row. A template means that the listed goods can normally be found somewhere in that settlement; it does not mean one merchant carries everything.

#### G — Gate-city regulated additions

All broad civilian equipment is available somewhere in the city. Pistol (250 GP), Musket (500 GP), Firearm Bullets (3 GP), Bomb (100 GP), Gunpowder Powder Horn (35 GP), and Gunpowder Keg (250 GP) require licenses, recorded purpose, or institutional purchase. #### Z — Dead-zone operating rule

Apply the appropriate mundane templates normally. Active magical functions, active enchantment, magical communication, and potions do not function while suppressed. Completed physical improvements, ordinary medicine, alchemy, tools, armor, and weapons continue to work. Stock should emphasize Healer's Kits, Antitoxin, Herbalism Kits, signal whistles, lanterns, locks, mechanical transport, standardized parts, and redundant mundane gear.

When the party asks for something not explicitly listed:

| Settlement type | DC 10 check | DC 15 check | DC 20 check |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gate city | Ordinary or specialist core equipment | Rare imported, high-value, or permit goods | Restricted contact or story lead; not automatic sale |
| Major city | Template goods | Specialist import or high-value order | Restricted institutional access |
| Minor city | Common/template goods | Relevant specialist good or regional import | Unusual high-value item, normally with delay |

Passing a check establishes availability, not permission. Restricted, illegal, or story-only equipment still requires the appropriate authority, relationship, or plot consequence.

### Dead zones and anti-magic areas

- Weapons, armor, tools, vehicles, medicine, antitoxin, acid, alchemist's fire, mundane poison, gunpowder, locks, and ordinary signals function normally.
- A Potion of Healing can be carried but its magic is suppressed inside Deepanvil or an active anti-magic area. It resumes functioning after leaving unless another effect damaged it.
- Arcane foci, druidic foci, holy symbols, component pouches, and spellbooks remain physical objects but cannot enable spellcasting where magic is suppressed.
- Permanently improved physical balance, alloy quality, reinforcement, sharpness, or structural strength remains.
- A weapon that needs active magic to burn, guide itself, communicate, levitate, repair itself, or project energy loses that active function.
- Deepanvil should sell more mundane medical and rescue stock, not less.

### Aetherite items

Do not give Aetherite crystals, resonance orbs, the Resonance Medallion, focus weapons, gate components, or Aetheronian devices a normal shop price. Existing lore treats them as restricted technology, plot evidence, strategic resources, or hidden infrastructure. Any legal sale, auction, or black-market lot should be individually staged and tracked.
