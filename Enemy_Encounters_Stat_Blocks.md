# Enemy Encounters Stat Blocks
**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Purpose:** Running compilation of enemies, guards, and NPCs for quick reference during play.

## Ravensport City Guard (Standard)

**Tier:** Common City Guard  
**Equivalent Class/Level:** Fighter 2–3 or Veteran (simplified)  
**AC** 16 (chain mail + shield)  
**HP** 22 (3d8 + 6)  
**Speed** 30 ft.

**Key Equipment**:
- Longsword or spear
- Shield
- Light crossbow
- Chain mail
- Uniform with Ravensport insignia

**Important Items**:
- City guard whistle (for calling backup)
- Manacles (2 pairs)
- 1d4 gp + 1d6 sp on average

**Possible Loot** (roll or choose 1–2):
- 2d6 cp
- Flask of cheap ale
- Stolen trinket (worth 5–10 gp)
- Letter from home or orders

## Elite Iron Talons / Gate Wardens

**Tier:** Elite Guard  
**Equivalent Class/Level:** Fighter 5–6 or Champion  
**AC** 18 (plate)  
**HP** 52 (7d8 + 21)  
**Speed** 30 ft.

**Key Equipment**:
- Longsword +1 (or masterwork)
- Heavy crossbow or early firearm
- Plate armor
- Shield (for some units)

**Important Items**:
- Gate Warden badge (magical identification)
- 1–2 healing potions (common)
- Aetherite crystal shard (small, non-functional) — rare
- 5d6 gp on average

**Possible Loot**:
- Healing potion
- Fine weapon or piece of armor
- Official orders or map
- Small pouch of rare metal dust

## Guard Captains / Commanders

**Tier:** Officer / Commander  
**Equivalent Class/Level:** Fighter 8–10 or Battle Master  
**AC** 18–20 (plate + shield or magic armor)  
**HP** 75–90  
**Speed** 30 ft.

**Key Equipment**:
- +1 Longsword or magical weapon
- Heavy crossbow or pistol
- Plate armor (possibly +1)
- Cloak of protection or similar

**Important Items**:
- Signet ring or badge of office
- 2–3 healing potions
- Scroll of *Sending* or similar (rare)
- 10d6+20 gp + gems

**Possible Loot**:
- Magical weapon or armor piece
- High-quality healing potions
- Important documents or maps
- Aetherite-related item (if high-ranking)

## General Guard Loot Table (Use for any guard encounter)

Roll 1d8 or choose:

1. Small amount of coin (1d10 gp)
2. Flask of alcohol or rations
3. Letter, orders, or personal note
4. Religious or family trinket
5. Weapon or piece of armor in good condition
6. Healing potion or minor potion
7. Contraband or suspicious item (Aetherbound related)
8. Nothing of value

## Monsters & Ecology

- **Common humanoids** like goblins and kobolds are **extremely rare** or nonexistent on this continent.
- Most "monsters" are either native wildlife or creatures that came through the gates long ago and adapted.
- **Dragons** exist but are **largely unknown** to most people. They avoid dead zones (the magic drain is uncomfortable even for them).
- There are **two known neutral dragons** on the continent. They don’t care much about the affairs of mortals or the Aetheron situation — they simply exist. They can be used for high-level side quests (e.g., a dragon slayer plot if players seek them out).

**General Monster Options** (random combat ideas):
- Giant spiders, wolves, or bears (wildlife)
- Undead (from old battles or necromancers)
- Elementals or planar creatures near gates
- Constructs or mechanical guardians (dwarven or Aetherite tech)
- Aberrations or mutated creatures near dead zones
