# Dead-Zone Creature Guidance

> **Repository role:** Supporting catalog guidance. `../../canon/DnD_Campaign_World_Bible.md` remains the authority for dead-zone mechanics.

## Authority rule

A creature entry may recommend a likely result, but it cannot override the World Bible's governing principles. Dead zones suppress active magic, sever magical connections, and drain magical beings. The Dungeon Master decides encounter-specific timing, collapse, recovery, restoration, and exceptions.

## Governing tests

1. **Physical viability:** Can the body remain alive or functional without continuing magical energy?
2. **Magical dependency:** Which traits, movement forms, defenses, or bodily functions require magic?
3. **Connection severance:** Which creator, summoner, scrying, command, tracking, home-object, or planar links end?
4. **Exposure time:** Does the result happen immediately, after drain, or only after prolonged isolation?
5. **Resonance exception:** Does an approved resonance or beacon-core field temporarily sustain the creature?

## Catalog recommendations

- Ordinary wildlife does not mutate merely because it enters a dead zone and normally functions without change.
- Approved nonmagical oozes are treated as unintelligent physical organisms unless the selected official version explicitly makes an effect magical.
- Physically viable creatures survive, while explicitly magical abilities and external magical connections are suppressed or severed.
- Fey, fiends, elementals, and similar magical beings follow the World Bible's drain rules. Individual entries record likely outcomes, but exact timing remains a DM decision.
- Magically animated objects and constructs lose active magical animation when their sustaining magic is unavailable. Whether shutdown is immediate or follows drain is encounter-specific.
- Magically sustained plants may wither or die when their physical life cannot continue without magic. The catalog does not establish a universal immediate-death rule.
- A separately approved nonmagical mechanical construct may function normally, but an official magical construct does not automatically become nonmagical merely because a similar chassis could be engineered.
- A functioning resonance or beacon-core field may sustain a magical entity only according to the approved field rules and DM adjudication.

## Known unresolved examples

The supplied catalog intentionally leaves several edge cases open, including Dryad survival duration, Redcap deterioration, Quickling speed loss, some spore effects, construct shutdown timing, and elemental collapse timing. These remain visible adjudication points rather than hidden assumptions.
