# Elyndria Random Encounter Roll Tables

## Mandatory Lookup Sequence

When a random encounter roll is requested:

1. Start in this roll-table section. Do **not** select a creature from the catalog first.
2. Use the requested category directly, or use [`Roll_Table_Selection_Guide.md`](Roll_Table_Selection_Guide.md) to choose the best table for a region or situation.
3. Roll 1d6 on the selected table.
4. Open every creature link in that result's **Catalog follow-up** column.
5. Use the creature catalog to check CR, recommended party level, regional fit, source baseline, dead-zone behavior, strengths, and weaknesses.
6. Open the owned official source for complete published statistics, or the custom combat repository when a campaign-specific stat block exists.

The roll table determines the initial encounter group. The creature catalog supplies the additional planning information. A catalog search must not replace the requested roll.

## Balance and Encounter Use

- These are encounter prompts, not guaranteed balanced combats.
- The rolled creatures may be distant signs, a social situation, a territorial warning, a pursuit, a hazard, or a combat encounter.
- Adjust distance, behavior, objectives, and creature count after consulting the catalog and the current party.
- The four gate-invasion tables represent campaign-scale forces. Players normally survive, evacuate, sabotage, protect a location, close a gate, or defeat a commander rather than destroy the entire force.

## Structured Data

- `data/roll_tables.json` is the synchronized machine-readable roll-table record.
- `data/roll_tables.csv` is the spreadsheet-friendly mirror.
- [`data/Roll_Table_Data_Schema.md`](data/Roll_Table_Data_Schema.md) defines the fields.
- `manifest.json` records counts, source-package identity, and checksums.
