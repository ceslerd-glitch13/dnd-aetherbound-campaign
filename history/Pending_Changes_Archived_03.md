# Pending Changes Archived 03 — Batch B Canon Synchronization

## Approval

Batch B was approved through direct user discussion and implemented as a synchronized canon package on 2026-07-16.

## Scope Implemented

### Dead zones, gods, and magical transport

- Dead zones block magical energy from entering, leaving, or remaining stable.
- Spellcasting, divine projection, divine observation, magical communication, teleportation, portals, planar travel, concentration, and active magic-item functions are suppressed without an approved resonance source.
- A deity outside the zone is unharmed at its source.
- A deity or true manifestation physically present inside experiences gradual drain and could theoretically lose godhood after extraordinary isolation.
- Teleportation and similar travel require every necessary endpoint to sustain magic.
- Completed physical changes that no longer require active magic remain. This wording was included to maintain consistency with the approved Batch C dead-zone clarification.
- A resonance orb does not automatically override a deliberate anti-magic field.

### Beacon-core resonance field

- An active beacon core produces a 20-foot supercharged field while harvesting magic for Aetheron.
- Constitution check: DC 10 + original spell level; cantrips DC 10.
- Failure produces wild magic or another DM-chosen unintended result and uses normal slot cost.
- Success halves slot level, rounded up; 1st-level spells use no slot.
- Spell level remains unchanged for all other rules.
- Duration doubles while maintained within the field and remaining duration is halved when the effect leaves.
- Damage and healing gain a flat 1d6 once per affected creature per casting.

### Aetherite crystal system and Resonance Medallion

- Standard portable Aetherite crystals have 24-charge capacity and regain 1 charge per hour in usable ambient magic.
- Rune-inscribed crystals define one spell or encoded function and independently support effects up to 3rd level.
- Unmarked orbs act as batteries and resonance sources.
- Spell energy costs 8 charges per level through the portable crystal system; cantrips cost no charges.
- The Resonance Medallion is only a housing for an unmarked orb.
- Its field is centered on the orb, has a 5-foot radius, costs 1 charge per hour in a dead zone, requires no attunement, and protects the holder plus adjacent creatures.
- Time protected by the orb does not create retroactive Magical Exhaustion.
- Ongoing magic loses support after leaving the field inside a dead zone.

### Veyne's three-crystal travel array

- The array uses one fully charged rune-inscribed transit crystal and two fully charged unmarked battery orbs.
- Each crystal contributes 24 charges, for 72 total.
- One Plane Shift-equivalent trip to Aetheron consumes 56 charges and leaves 16.
- Two crystals provide only 48 charges and cannot activate the formula.
- The stolen Resonance Medallion orb was intended as one of the two batteries.
- The transit inscription can encode a complex effect but cannot produce it independently above the crystal's 3rd-level standalone output.

### Ravensport government and Captain Rurik

- Elara Voss is Lord Protector and City Council chair.
- Harlan Voss is the retired former Lord Protector.
- Seraphine Vale oversees strategic gate security.
- Vorn Ironvein represents trade and logistics.
- Lirael Starveil leads the Arcane Wardens.
- Thorne Blackwood commands ordinary city law enforcement.
- Kael Thorne is operational Gate Commander and commands the Iron Talons under Seraphine.
- Kael Thorne and Thorne Blackwood are unrelated.
- Iron Talons are standardized at 200, four companies of 50.
- Captain Elandor "Blackscale" Rurik is the sole Captain Rurik, owner of The Rusty Anchor, semi-retired ship captain, and starter-quest giver.
- Captain Rurik Voss was removed.

### Terminology

- Aetheron — realm/world and civilization when context is clear.
- Aetheronian/Aetheronians — person or people of Aetheron.
- Aetherite — crystal material and broader technology.
- Veyne's organization — neutral name for the still-unnamed secret organization.
- Aetherbound — retained only as the repository/campaign working title.
- Aetherian/Aetherians and "the Aetherites" as a people were retired.
