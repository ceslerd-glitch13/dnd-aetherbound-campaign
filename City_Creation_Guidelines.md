# City Creation Guidelines (Mandatory Reference)

**Purpose**: This is the primary mandatory reference for creating or expanding any city in the campaign. These rules ensure consistency, immersion, and immediate playability.

---

## Important Scope Clarification

This guideline applies **only** when the user explicitly requests a **city** or **large settlement** (e.g., town, capital, or major hub).  

If the request is for an **individual building, mansion, stronghold, tower, keep, or similar smaller location**, **do not** apply the full city creation rules. Instead, create a functional, concise solution based directly on the user’s prompt. Focus on named owner, key staff, important rooms/features, defenses, and 2–3 immediate plot hooks. Keep it lightweight and table-ready.

---

## Mandatory Requirements for Every City

### 1. Leadership
- 1 Primary Ruler (name + title + brief role + alignment)
- 3–5 Key Council / Figureheads (name + title + brief role + alignment)

### 2. Key Locations & Named Proprietors
- Shops: Minimum 4–6 distinct shops, each with a named shopkeep (first and last name or title + name)
- Inns / Taverns: Minimum 2 named inns/taverns with short descriptions

### 3. City Structure
- District Breakdown with clear purposes and atmosphere
- Economy Notes (major exports/imports, black market presence)
- Basic Services
- Defensive Measures (beyond walls — guards, elite forces, patrols, magical protections)

### 4. Immersion & Playability
- 3–5 immediate plot hooks tied to the city
- Ongoing life and tensions
- Interaction with dead zones, gates, and other races

### 5. Cross-Referencing
- Always check World Bible Sections 4 and 5
- Reference Guideline_World_Update_Change_Management.md when recording changes
- Reference Guideline_NPC_Character_Creation.md for building named characters

### 6. Output Standards
- Concise and direct
- Clear headings and bullet points for fast table reference
- Every named NPC should have enough detail to run immediately

---

**Enforcement Note**: These rules exist because generic city descriptions break immersion. Every new city must meet these minimum standards before being considered complete.
