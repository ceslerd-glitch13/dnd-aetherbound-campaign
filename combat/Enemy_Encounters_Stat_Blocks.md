# Enemy Encounters & Stat Blocks

**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Purpose:** Quick-reference combat statistics for guards, criminals, Veyne's personnel, constructs, and creatures likely to appear during the starter arc.

## Use Rules

- Combat is a possible resolution, not the assumed resolution.
- Use only the enemies justified by the characters' actions and current quest.
- Most criminals prefer escape, surrender, bribery, or negotiation over death.
- Veyne's trained personnel protect evidence and silence witnesses once those witnesses know enough to threaten an operation.
- Do not prepare large later-campaign rosters until the players choose a quest and their level, party size, and approach are known.
- Adjust enemy numbers before altering core statistics.
- Dead-zone and anti-magic changes are included in each relevant entry.
- Permanent physical improvements remain; active magical functions are suppressed when their sustaining magic is unavailable.

# Encounter Scaling Guide

These examples assume approximately four characters.

| Party level | Routine encounter | Dangerous encounter |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 Dockside Lookouts + 2 Smugglers | 1 Smuggler Enforcer + 2 Smugglers |
| 2 | 1 Silent Hand Operative + 2 Lookouts | 1 Survey Construct + 2 Smugglers |
| 3 | 1 Retrieval Specialist + 2 Lookouts | 2 Silent Hand Operatives + 1 Enforcer |
| 4 | 1 Marked Gate Beast | 1 Retrieval Specialist + 2 Silent Hand Operatives |

Use guards mainly as social or legal complications. A fight against lawful city defenders should create serious consequences.

# Civil and Gate Security

## Ravensport City Guard

**CR:** 1/2  
**Encounter Role:** Law-enforcement line defender  
**AC:** 16 (chain mail, shield)  
**HP:** 22 (4d8 + 4)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Longsword:** +4 to hit, 5 ft.; 6 (1d8 + 2) slashing.
- **Spear:** +4 to hit, 5 ft. or range 20/60; 5 (1d6 + 2) piercing, or 6 (1d8 + 2) when used with two hands.
- **Light Crossbow:** +3 to hit, range 80/320; 5 (1d8 + 1) piercing.

**Abilities**
- **Guard Formation:** While adjacent to another conscious guard, the guard has advantage on checks and saves against being shoved or knocked prone.
- **Interpose — Reaction:** The guard grants +2 AC against one attack targeting an adjacent creature. The guard must be able to see the attacker and be carrying a shield.
- **Call Backup:** A guard whistle can normally bring additional patrols within several rounds in populated districts.

**Tactics**
- Guards issue commands, attempt restraint, and use nonlethal melee damage when practical.
- They form a shield line and wait for reinforcements rather than chasing into obvious ambushes.
- They use lethal force against deadly threats, active gate breaches, or attackers who continue after clear warnings.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- No statistical change to mundane weapons or armor.
- Magical badges, communication effects, or active enchantments are suppressed.
- Guards switch to whistles, runners, physical signals, and drilled formations.

**Important Items**
- Guard whistle, two pairs of manacles, patrol orders, 1d4 gp and 1d6 sp.

## Iron Talon Gate Defender

**CR:** 3  
**Encounter Role:** Elite defender and containment soldier  
**AC:** 18 (plate)  
**HP:** 52 (7d8 + 21)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two melee attacks.
- **Masterwork Longsword:** +6 to hit, 5 ft.; 8 (1d8 + 4) slashing.
- **Heavy Crossbow:** +4 to hit, range 100/400; 7 (1d10 + 2) piercing.
- **Shield Bash — Bonus Action:** +6 to hit, 5 ft.; 6 (1d4 + 4) bludgeoning, and the target must succeed on a DC 14 Strength save or be knocked prone.

**Abilities**
- **Hold the Line — Reaction:** When a creature within reach moves away without Disengaging, the defender makes one longsword attack. On a hit, the target's speed becomes 0 for the rest of the turn.
- **Containment Discipline:** Advantage on saving throws against fear and effects that would force movement.
- **Heart Training:** The defender ignores disadvantage caused only by loud alarms, smoke, poor visibility from emergency systems, or crowded defensive corridors.

**Tactics**
- Talons protect exits, isolate the most dangerous intruder, and prevent movement deeper into the Heart.
- They use shield bashes to control position and crossbows against flying or distant targets.
- They prioritize containment over pursuit outside their assigned defensive area.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- No change to the masterwork physical weapon, armor, or tactics.
- Magical badge functions and any active weapon enchantment are suppressed.
- A permanently refined weapon remains well balanced and durable but deals no magical bonus damage.

**Important Items**
- Gate badge, healing potion, sealed orders, 2d6 gp.

## Guard Captain or District Commander

**CR:** 5  
**Encounter Role:** Tactical leader  
**AC:** 19 (plate, shield)  
**HP:** 85 (10d8 + 40)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two weapon attacks.
- **Longsword:** +7 to hit, 5 ft.; 8 (1d8 + 4) slashing.
- **Heavy Crossbow or Pistol:** +6 to hit, range 100/400 or 40/120; 8 (1d10 + 3) piercing.

**Abilities**
- **Command Ally — Bonus Action, 3/Day:** One allied guard within 30 feet may move up to half its speed or make one weapon attack.
- **Parry — Reaction:** The captain adds +3 AC against one melee attack that would hit.
- **Steady Command:** Allied guards within 20 feet have advantage on saves against fear while the captain is conscious.

**Tactics**
- Coordinates guards rather than seeking single combat.
- Orders arrests when possible and focuses attacks on anyone threatening civilians or the Heart.
- Withdraws to a defensible position if isolated.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- Mundane command abilities remain.
- Active magical equipment, communication, or protective effects are suppressed.

## Dockside Lookout

**CR:** 1/8  
**Encounter Role:** Alarm, scout, or fleeing witness  
**AC:** 13  
**HP:** 9 (2d8)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Dagger:** +3 to hit, 5 ft. or range 20/60; 3 (1d4 + 1) piercing.
- **Sling:** +3 to hit, range 30/120; 3 (1d4 + 1) bludgeoning.

**Abilities**
- **Raise the Alarm:** As an action, the lookout whistles, rings a bell, or shouts. Nearby allies become aware of intruders.
- **Street Escape — Bonus Action:** The lookout Disengages or Dashes. Once used, this ability cannot be used again until the lookout spends a turn hidden or out of sight.

**Tactics**
- Avoids direct combat, warns allies, and runs.
- Surrenders readily if trapped and offered reasonable treatment.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- No change.

**Possible Clues**
- Chalk route marks, a copied warehouse token, 1d6 sp, or a message naming a meeting place without naming the organizer.

# Veyne's Organization

## Silent Hand Operative

**CR:** 2  
**Encounter Role:** Witness suppressor and infiltration specialist  
**AC:** 15 (reinforced coat)  
**HP:** 45 (7d8 + 14)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two short-blade attacks.
- **Short Blade:** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; 6 (1d6 + 3) piercing.
- **Hand Crossbow:** +5 to hit, range 30/120; 6 (1d6 + 3) piercing.
- **Sedative Bolt, 1/Day:** On a hand-crossbow hit, the target must succeed on a DC 13 Constitution save or be poisoned until the end of its next turn and have its speed reduced to 0 during that time.

**Abilities**
- **Silence the Witness:** The operative has advantage on its first attack against a creature that is surprised, restrained, or isolated from all conscious allies by at least 15 feet.
- **Smoke Capsule, 1/Day:** Creates a 10-foot-radius heavily obscured cloud until the end of the operative's next turn.
- **Destroy Evidence — Bonus Action:** The operative may ignite or shatter one unattended Small document, crystal, or fragile device within reach.

**Tactics**
- Attempts bribery or intimidation before the target understands too much.
- Once ordered to eliminate witnesses, isolates one target, uses the sedative bolt, destroys evidence, and withdraws.
- Does not surrender easily, but may do so if capture offers the only chance of survival.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- Fully functional; its core tools are mechanical and alchemical.
- Any magical disguise, communication, or escape item is suppressed.

**Possible Clues**
- Coded orders, a false identity, route map, poison vial, or a payment token connected to Silas.

## Aetherite Retrieval Specialist

**CR:** 4  
**Encounter Role:** Elite evidence recovery and targeted elimination  
**AC:** 17 (mechanically reinforced armor)  
**HP:** 76 (9d8 + 36)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two attacks with the resonance blade or one blade attack and one projector attack.
- **Resonance Blade:** +6 to hit, 5 ft.; 7 (1d8 + 3) slashing plus 3 (1d6) force.
- **Aetherite Projector:** +6 to hit, range 60/180; 12 (2d8 + 3) force.

**Abilities**
- **Suppression Charge, Recharge 5–6:** The specialist throws a charge to a point within 30 feet. Active magical effects in a 10-foot-radius sphere are suppressed until the end of the specialist's next turn. This is a short disruption, not a dead zone.
- **Objective Priority:** The specialist has advantage on checks to locate, seize, or identify an Aetherite object or campaign evidence it has been ordered to recover.
- **Secure the Asset — Bonus Action:** The specialist may attempt to disarm a creature within 5 feet. The target makes a DC 14 Strength or Dexterity save, losing one held item of the specialist's choice on failure.
- **Reactive Barrier — Reaction, 2/Day:** Reduce damage from one attack by 8.

**Tactics**
- Targets the orb, documents, witnesses, or prototype before attacking indiscriminately.
- Uses suppression to disable magical defenses, then disarms the holder.
- If the objective is secured, it withdraws rather than continuing unnecessary combat.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- Resonance blade loses its extra force damage.
- Projector fires a physical penetrator instead: +6 to hit, 8 (1d10 + 3) piercing.
- Suppression charge and Reactive Barrier are unavailable.
- AC, physical blade, and Objective Priority remain.

**Possible Loot**
- Mechanically refined weapon, damaged projector, coded retrieval order, secure evidence case, or nonfunctional Aetherite fragment.

## Silas Crowe

**CR:** 5  
**Encounter Role:** Social controller, mobile duelist, and field coordinator  
**AC:** 16 (concealed armor)  
**HP:** 82 (11d8 + 33)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two concealed-blade or hand-crossbow attacks.
- **Concealed Blade:** +7 to hit, 5 ft.; 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing.
- **Hand Crossbow:** +7 to hit, range 30/120; 7 (1d6 + 4) piercing.
- **Exploit Opening:** Once per turn, Silas deals an extra 7 (2d6) damage to a target adjacent to one of Silas's allies or affected by a condition.

**Abilities**
- **Command Bodyguard — Bonus Action:** One allied operative within 30 feet may move up to half speed or make one weapon attack.
- **Calculated Retreat — Reaction, 3/Day:** When a creature ends its turn within 5 feet of Silas, he moves up to half speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Smoke and Mirrors, 1/Day:** Silas creates a 15-foot-radius smoke cloud centered on himself and immediately moves up to his speed.
- **Read the Room:** Silas has advantage on Insight checks and on Deception checks made to conceal who he serves or why he wants an object.

**Tactics**
- Begins with negotiation, misinformation, or an offer.
- Orders subordinates to recover evidence and prevent escape while he stays mobile.
- If defeat appears likely, he withdraws and leaves subordinates to delay pursuit.
- If elimination has been authorized, he focuses on the best-informed witness first.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- No change to core combat abilities; his equipment is primarily physical and alchemical.
- Any hidden magical communication or tracking item is suppressed.

**Important Items**
- Formal contracts, coded payment records, blue-crystal signet ring, concealed weapon, 4d10 gp.

## Beacon Operator

**CR:** 3  
**Encounter Role:** Controller who exploits a beacon-core field  
**AC:** 15  
**HP:** 58 (9d8 + 18)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Two staff attacks, or one staff attack and one Resonance Bolt while within a powered field.
- **Reinforced Staff:** +5 to hit, 5 ft.; 6 (1d6 + 3) bludgeoning.
- **Resonance Bolt:** +5 to hit, range 60 ft.; 9 (2d8) force. Available only within a usable resonance field.

**Abilities**
- **Core Channeler:** While within 20 feet of an active beacon core, the operator gains 5 temporary HP at the start of its turn if it has none.
- **Field Step — Bonus Action, Recharge 4–6:** Teleport up to 20 feet to an unoccupied space inside the same beacon field.
- **Core Surge, 1/Day:** Creatures of the operator's choice within 10 feet make a DC 13 Constitution save, taking 14 (4d6) force damage on failure or half on success.
- **Mechanical Shutdown Knowledge:** The operator has advantage on checks to activate, disable, or sabotage beacon regulators.

**Tactics**
- Remains inside the core's 20-foot field.
- Uses machinery and guardians to keep enemies outside while firing bolts.
- Attempts to overload or destroy evidence if capture is unavoidable.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- Outside a powered field, Resonance Bolt, Field Step, Core Surge, and temporary HP are unavailable.
- The operator relies on staff attacks, tools, cover, and escape routes.

# Lord Cassian Veyne — Early Encounter Contingency

This entry exists because an open-world party may locate or attack the primary antagonist far earlier than expected. He is not intended as a balanced starter encounter.

## Lord Cassian Veyne

**CR:** 15  
**Encounter Role:** Mobile boss, commander, and overwhelming early-campaign threat  
**AC:** 19 (integrated armor and reactive field)  
**HP:** 210 (20d8 + 120)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

**Saving Throws:** Dex +8, Con +10, Int +10, Wis +8, Cha +9  
**Skills:** Arcana +10, Deception +9, Insight +8, Investigation +10, Perception +8  
**Damage Resistances:** Force, psychic  
**Condition Resistances:** Charmed, frightened  
**Senses:** Passive Perception 18  
**Languages:** Common, Dwarvish, Elvish, and Aetheronian technical notation

**Attacks**
- **Multiattack:** Three attacks in any combination with Resonance Blade or Aetherite Sidearm.
- **Resonance Blade:** +10 to hit, 5 ft.; 14 (2d8 + 5) slashing plus 4 (1d8) force.
- **Aetherite Sidearm:** +10 to hit, range 80/240; 18 (3d8 + 5) force.
- **Null Lance, Recharge 5–6:** Veyne fires a 60-foot-long, 5-foot-wide line. Creatures in the line make a DC 18 Dexterity save, taking 36 (8d8) force damage on failure or half on success. Active magical effects on a failed target are suppressed until the end of that target's next turn.

**Abilities**
- **Legendary Resistance, 2/Day:** If Veyne fails a saving throw, he may choose to succeed.
- **Command Array — Bonus Action:** One ally within 60 feet may move up to half speed and make one weapon attack.
- **Reactive Barrier — Reaction:** Reduce damage from one source by 20.
- **Emergency Transit, 1/Day:** While usable magic is present, Veyne teleports up to 120 feet to a prepared anchor he can see or has previously keyed. This fails in a dead zone, inside a deliberate anti-magic field, or if the anchor is disabled.
- **Master of the Operation:** Veyne has advantage on initiative and cannot be surprised while one of his prepared surveillance devices or conscious operatives is within 60 feet.

**Legendary Actions — 3 per round**
- **Advance:** Move up to half speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
- **Blade or Sidearm:** Make one attack.
- **Disrupting Pulse — Costs 2 Actions:** One creature within 30 feet makes a DC 18 Constitution save or takes 13 (3d8) force damage and cannot take reactions until the start of its next turn.

**Tactics**
- Veyne does not remain for a fair fight. He protects critical technology, removes informed witnesses, and leaves once the objective is secured.
- He gives uninformed adventurers one opportunity to withdraw or accept a deal when secrecy can still be preserved.
- Once the party possesses critical knowledge, he or his subordinates may attempt capture, memory control through outside resources, or elimination.
- In an early encounter, provide believable routes for retreat, surrender, distraction, or environmental escape. Do not secretly weaken Veyne merely to make an impulsive attack safe.
- If the party defeats or kills him through exceptional planning, luck, alliances, or exploitation of a dead zone, accept the result and restructure the campaign around the power vacuum and unfinished Aetheron operation.

**Dead-Zone / Anti-Magic Variant**
- AC becomes 18.
- Resonance Blade loses its extra force damage.
- Aetherite Sidearm switches to a physical penetrator: +10 to hit, 14 (2d8 + 5) piercing.
- Null Lance, Reactive Barrier, Emergency Transit, and Disrupting Pulse are unavailable.
- Legendary Resistance, command skill, physical attacks, movement, armor, and tactical knowledge remain.
- His effective threat is reduced but remains far above starter level.

**Important Items**
- Integrated Aetherite devices that may be damaged or locked on defeat.
- Encrypted operational records.
- High-ranking signet and multiple false identities.
- A keyed transit anchor or technical component, depending on encounter location.

# General Loot and Evidence

Avoid treating lawful guards as routine loot sources. Loot from criminals and Veyne's personnel should favor evidence over treasure.

Roll 1d8 or choose:

1. Coded route note or meeting time.
2. Forged permit, badge, or cargo seal.
3. 2d10 gp in mixed coin.
4. Mechanical bypass tool.
5. Alchemical smoke or sedative dose.
6. Damaged Aetherite fragment with no independent power.
7. Payment token linked to an intermediary.
8. Personal letter revealing fear, coercion, or limited knowledge.

# Dead-Zone Encounter Rules

Use the World Bible as the authority.

- Spellcasting and active magic-item functions fail without an approved resonance source.
- Concentration and active ongoing effects end or become suppressed when their sustaining magic cannot remain connected.
- Magical movement, teleportation, summoning, portals, and magical escape abilities fail when a required endpoint is unprotected.
- Elementals, fey, fiends, projected avatars, and similar magical beings have disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls and take 1d6 force damage per minute outside combat or per round during combat, unless a creature-specific entry says otherwise.
- Permanent physical improvements do not reverse merely because an object enters the zone; active magical enhancements are suppressed.
- Divine projection, direct observation, miracles, and active divine abilities are blocked.
- A charged resonance orb can create a 5-foot exception, but it does not automatically bypass a deliberate anti-magic field.

# Beacon-Core Encounter Field

Within 20 feet of an active beacon core:

- Spellcasting is possible despite the surrounding dead zone.
- Each cast requires a Constitution check: DC 10 + original spell level; cantrips DC 10.
- Failure creates wild magic or another DM-chosen unintended outcome and uses the normal slot cost.
- Success halves slot cost, rounded up; 1st-level spells use no slot.
- The spell keeps its original level for all other rules.
- Duration is doubled while the relevant effect remains within the field; halve remaining duration when it leaves.
- Damaging spells add 1d6 force damage once per affected creature per casting.
- Healing spells add 1d6 healing once per affected creature per casting.

# Monsters and Ecology

- Common humanoids such as goblins and kobolds are extremely rare or nonexistent on this continent.
- Most monsters are native wildlife or creatures that came through the gates and adapted.
- Starter encounters should favor criminals, constructs, planar animals, altered wildlife, and gate-incursion creatures.
- Add new creatures only when a chosen quest requires them.
