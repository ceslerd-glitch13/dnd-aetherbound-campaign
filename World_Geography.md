# World Geography — Elyndria

**Continent Name:** Elyndria  
**Map Scope:** Continental overview  
**Approximate Scale:** The map scale bar uses 0–200 km intervals. Elyndria is approximately 900–1,000 km east to west and 650–750 km north to south, allowing for the stylized coastline.  
**Primary Visual Reference:** The Elyndria world map generated for this campaign.

---

## 1. Canonical Map Interpretation

The world map is the primary visual reference for the **relative position** of terrain, settlements, roads, rivers, ports, and dead zones. Because the map was AI-generated, the following rules prevent visual artifacts from becoming accidental canon:

- The settlement lists in the **World Bible** remain authoritative for the continent's 15 major cities and 20 minor cities.
- Each canonical settlement exists only once, even if a name is accidentally repeated on the map.
- Small repeated or altered labels near dead zones are treated as unnamed local outposts, holds, mines, or watch posts unless they are formally added to the World Bible.
- The ten dead-zone names and placements in this file are authoritative when a map label is missing, duplicated, or slightly misplaced.
- Deepanvil is canonically located **inside** the Deepanvil Dead Zone, including its underground mining network, even if the faded boundary on a particular map rendering does not fully cover the city icon.
- Roads and rivers should follow the map's intended routes rather than minor rendering gaps.
- Formal political borders are not yet fixed. The map shows cultural influence and settlement patterns rather than sovereign national boundaries.

## 2. Overall Shape and Borders

Elyndria is a broad, irregular continent surrounded by two named seas:

- **The Frost Sea** borders the western and northwestern coast. Its waters are cold, rough, and influenced by the northern mountain climate.
- **The Azure Sea** borders the eastern and southeastern coast. It carries most overseas shipping connected to Ravensport and Havenport.

The continent is framed by:

- a long, snowbound mountain belt across the north;
- a steep western mountain spine running nearly the full length of the continent;
- broad central forests divided by rivers and trade roads;
- warmer southern plains, mixed woodland, marshes, and deeply indented coasts;
- a heavily traveled eastern river mouth and harbor system centered on Ravensport.

The northern and western mountain ranges have not yet received formal canonical names. Until named, they should be referred to descriptively as the **Northern Mountains** and **Western Mountains**.

## 3. Major Geographic Regions

### Northern Crown

The far north is a frozen and alpine region composed of snowfields, tundra, steep valleys, and the Northern Mountains.

- **Ironforge** occupies a major mountain pass and serves as the northern dwarven gate city.
- **Stonefist**, **Goldhammer**, **Runeforge**, **Copperhold**, **Silvervein**, and **Ironpeak** form a connected dwarven industrial and trade belt.
- **Frostwood Wastes**, **Shiverstep Basin**, and portions of **Greyfen Moor** make travel through the north and northeast dangerous or inconvenient.
- Elven settlement is limited, although forest communities such as Silvandar and Thalorien occupy the milder southern slopes and river valleys.
- Winter conditions restrict travel, close minor passes, and increase dependence on the fortified major roads.

### Western Mountain Spine

The western edge of Elyndria is dominated by high mountains, mineral-rich valleys, mines, and dwarven holds.

- **Khazadun** and **Gemdeep** occupy the upper western mountains.
- **Deepanvil** lies farther south within its own magic dead zone and is the region's largest mining and engineering center.
- **Forgehill**, **Anvilrest**, **Stonewatch**, and **Hammerfall** form the southern continuation of the western dwarven settlement chain.
- Roads in this region follow valleys and mountain shelves. Cross-country travel away from the roads is slow and hazardous.
- Deepanvil's mines extend beneath the visible surface settlement and reach toward an unknown Aetheron node or beacon.

### Lake Aelorin Basin

**Lake Aelorin** lies east of the Western Mountains and acts as the principal freshwater source for the central continent.

- Meltwater and mountain rivers feed the lake from the north and west.
- **Westlake Port** sits on the lake's southeastern side and functions as an inland shipping, fishing, and transfer settlement. It is not currently counted among the World Bible's 35 canonical cities.
- The lake's eastern outflow becomes Elyndria's main eastbound river.
- The basin connects Deepanvil and the western mining settlements to the central elven regions by road and water.
- The Westlake Wilds Dead Zone lies southeast of the lake within the central forest belt.

### Central Forest Belt

The central band contains Elyndria's greatest continuous forests, fertile river valleys, and largest concentration of elven settlements.

- **Faerindel** stands near the center of the continent on the main river and is the principal elven gate city.
- **Silvandar** and **Thalorien** occupy the northern forest belt.
- **Whisperleaf**, **Moonshadow**, **Starbloom**, **Riverglade**, **Hillwhisper**, **Forestveil**, **Dawnspire**, and **Eldergrove** form a network of smaller forest and river settlements.
- **Eloweth**, **Lirael**, and **Arvandor** anchor the southern and eastern sides of the elven heartland.
- Roads are less rigid than dwarven highways and often curve around old forest growth, rivers, and dead zones.
- River travel, forestry, agriculture, magical craft, and trade between the three gate cities make this the most interconnected region of Elyndria.

### Eastern Riverlands and Ravensport Coast

The central river broadens as it flows east through Riverglade and Eldergrove toward Ravensport.

- **Ravensport** occupies the eastern coast where the river reaches the Azure Sea.
- The lowlands west of Ravensport contain branching channels, wetlands, bridges, and heavily used trade roads.
- Ravensport's harbor is divided into exactly four principal piers:
  1. North Pier
  2. East Pier
  3. South Pier
  4. Merchants' Pier
- The nearest dead zone is approximately 30 minutes away by a fast horse or about two hours on foot, depending on the exact gate and route used.
- Thornhollow lies southwest of Ravensport, while Greyfen Moor lies farther north in the eastern interior.

### Southern Plains and Coast

Southern Elyndria is warmer and more open than the central and northern regions.

- **Newbridge** is the major inland city and road junction of the south-central region.
- **Crossroads**, **Tradehaven**, and **Borderwatch** organize much of the southern road network.
- **Unityport** serves the southwestern coast.
- **Havenport** serves the southeastern coast and the lower Azure Sea trade routes.
- The region includes grasslands, light forests, river valleys, coastal inlets, and broad wetlands.
- Four dead zones—Sunken Vale, The Withered Shore, Duskmarsh, and Silent Fen—complicate southern settlement and trade.

## 4. Water Systems

### Lake Aelorin and the Main River

Elyndria's principal river system begins in the Western Mountains and Lake Aelorin.

1. Mountain runoff flows into **Lake Aelorin**.
2. The main river leaves the lake on its eastern side near Westlake Port.
3. It flows east through the central forest belt and reaches the Faerindel region.
4. It continues through or beside **Riverglade** and **Eldergrove**.
5. The river reaches **Ravensport**, where it forms a navigable river mouth and harbor before emptying into the Azure Sea.

This route is continuous and should always be shown as the main west-to-east waterway of the continent.

### Northern Tributaries

Streams and rivers descend from the Northern Mountains near Ironforge, Goldhammer, Silvervein, and Runeforge.

- Some feed Lake Aelorin.
- Others join the central river north or east of Faerindel.
- Seasonal snowmelt makes northern waterways strongest in spring and early summer.

### Southern Drainage

Several river branches flow south from the central highlands and forest belt.

- Western and central branches pass near Dawnspire, Crossroads, and Newbridge.
- Southeastern channels drain toward Tradehaven, Havenport, Silent Fen, and nearby coastal inlets.
- Marshlands slow travel and make bridges, ferries, and maintained fords strategically important.

### Coasts and Harbors

- Ravensport is the largest eastern harbor and the principal dimensional and overseas trade port.
- Havenport is the main southeastern seaport.
- Unityport is the main southwestern seaport.
- Westlake Port is an inland lake and river port.
- The northern and western coasts are colder, rockier, and less developed than the Azure Sea coast.

## 6. Roads, Trade Routes, and Chokepoints

### Northern Mountain Road

A fortified east-west route links:

**Stonefist → Ironforge → Goldhammer → Runeforge → Ironpeak**

Branches descend toward Copperhold, Silvervein, Silvandar, and Thalorien. Mountain passes near Ironforge and Runeforge are major strategic chokepoints.

### Western Mining Road

A north-south route follows the Western Mountains:

**Khazadun → Gemdeep → Deepanvil → Anvilrest → Stonewatch → Hammerfall → Unityport**

Forgehill and Lake Aelorin connect to this route through eastern branches. Deepanvil's dead zone and the limited mountain passes make this road difficult to replace.

### Central Gate Road

The most important transcontinental land route links the three gate-city regions:

**Ironforge region → Faerindel → Ravensport**

Western branches connect Faerindel to Lake Aelorin and Deepanvil. Eastern branches parallel the main river through Riverglade and Eldergrove before entering Ravensport.

### Southern Trade Road

The southern trunk route links:

**Unityport → Crossroads → Newbridge → Tradehaven → Havenport**

Borderwatch connects by a southern branch. Additional roads turn north toward Lirael, Arvandor, Faerindel, and Ravensport.

### Minor Roads

Minor roads and forest paths connect the elven settlements. They usually:

- follow rivers and natural clearings;
- curve around old-growth forest;
- avoid dead zones unless no practical alternative exists;
- connect smaller settlements to a nearby major city rather than crossing the entire continent.

### Strategic Geography

Important chokepoints include:

- the mountain passes around Ironforge and Runeforge;
- the Lake Aelorin outlet and Westlake Port;
- bridges and crossings around Faerindel, Newbridge, and the eastern riverlands;
- the Ravensport river mouth and harbor;
- narrow safe corridors between southern dead zones;
- the mountain roads serving Deepanvil.

## 7. Magic Dead Zones — Exactly 10

All ten dead zones are approximately city-sized, visually represented by faded gray patches, and suppress magic according to the World Bible's current rules. Their boundaries may extend underground or beyond the visible map shading.

| # | Dead Zone | Placement and Geographic Effect |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | **Deepanvil** | Central-west Western Mountains. Contains Deepanvil and portions of its mines. The city uses the zone as protection from hostile magic and relies on engineering. |
| 2 | **Westlake Wilds** | Southeast of Lake Aelorin, west of Lirael and south of the central Faerindel region. It disrupts direct forest travel and pushes roads around its edge. |
| 3 | **Frostwood Wastes** | North-central interior between the northern dwarven belt and the upper elven forests. Cold, isolated, and crossed only by minor routes. |
| 4 | **Shiverstep Basin** | Far northeastern highlands beyond Runeforge. Snowbound and remote, with little effect on ordinary continental trade. |
| 5 | **Greyfen Moor** | Northeastern/eastern interior between Ironpeak, Thalorien, Eldergrove, and the upper Ravensport approaches. Roads bend around its wet ground. |
| 6 | **Thornhollow** | East-southeast interior, southwest of Ravensport and north of Havenport. A nearby keep or watch settlement monitors the safe routes around it. |
| 7 | **Silent Fen** | Far southeastern lowlands west of Havenport. Wetlands and magical suppression make the area difficult to cross. |
| 8 | **Duskmarsh** | South-central wetlands between Newbridge, Borderwatch, and the southeastern routes. Contains or borders small local settlements and watch posts. |
| 9 | **Sunken Vale** | Southwestern interior north of Unityport and east of Hammerfall. Roads from the western mountains divert around or skirt the zone. |
| 10 | **The Withered Shore** | Southwestern coast east of Unityport. The dead zone reaches the coast and creates an isolated, blighted shoreline. |

### Dead-Zone Route Effects

- Most major roads curve around dead zones because the detour is usually safer and faster.
- A few local roads enter zones to reach mines, engineering settlements, criminal refuges, or watch posts.
- Deepanvil is the only established major city intentionally centered inside a dead zone.
- Several small settlements and outposts exist on the edges of, or partially within, dead zones as shown on the map.
- Two or three zones—especially Shiverstep Basin and remote portions of Frostwood Wastes—have little direct effect on major continental trade.

## 8. Climate and Seasonal Travel

### North and High Mountains

- Long winters, heavy snow, and short cool summers.
- Minor roads and passes may close during severe winter weather.
- Spring snowmelt increases river flow and landslide risk.

### Central Forests and Riverlands

- Temperate climate with distinct spring, summer, autumn, and winter seasons.
- Reliable rainfall supports forests, agriculture, and river travel.
- Flooding is most likely during spring snowmelt and heavy seasonal rain.

### Southern Plains and Coasts

- Warmer summers and milder winters.
- Longer growing seasons support human-majority farming and trade communities.
- Marshes become harder to cross during wet seasons.
- Coastal storms can disrupt shipping to Unityport and Havenport.

### Eastern Coast

- Maritime weather moderated by the Azure Sea.
- Ravensport remains active year-round, although storms and river flooding periodically slow harbor traffic.

## 9. Approximate Travel Planning

These are planning estimates derived from the map scale, not fixed encounter-by-encounter rules.

### Standard Assumptions

- **On foot, maintained road:** 25–30 km per day
- **Mounted, maintained road:** 40–55 km per day
- **Fast horse for a short urgent journey:** 65–80 km per day equivalent, but not sustainable over multiple days
- **Mountain, marsh, forest, or dead-zone route:** reduce progress by 25–50%
- **River travel downstream:** often faster than road travel when boats and ports are available

### Approximate Major Journeys

| Route | Approximate Distance | Foot Travel | Mounted Travel |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Ironforge to Faerindel | 300–350 km | 11–14 days | 6–8 days |
| Faerindel to Ravensport | 350–400 km | 13–16 days | 7–9 days |
| Deepanvil to Faerindel | 450–500 km | 17–20 days | 9–12 days |
| Faerindel to Newbridge | 300–350 km | 11–14 days | 6–8 days |
| Newbridge to Havenport | 300–350 km | 11–14 days | 6–8 days |
| Newbridge to Ravensport | 450–550 km | 17–22 days | 10–13 days |
| Ravensport to its nearest dead zone | Local route | About 2 hours | About 30 minutes at a fast pace |

Weather, road quality, caravans, encounters, and avoidance of dead zones may lengthen these journeys.

## 10. Geographic Effects on Culture, Trade, and War

- Dwarven power is concentrated in the northern and western mountains, where mines, passes, and durable roads favor engineering and fortified settlements.
- Elven power is concentrated in the central forest belt and river valleys, where communities are connected by waterways, woodland paths, and the Faerindel gate.
- Human and mixed settlements dominate the warmer southern plains, major crossroads, and coastal ports.
- Ravensport controls the most important eastern harbor and the main river's access to the Azure Sea.
- Newbridge controls a key southern river and road crossing.
- Ironforge controls access through the northern mountains.
- Deepanvil's dead zone protects its industry from many magical threats but makes magical healing, transport, and communication unavailable inside the city.
- Dead zones fragment otherwise direct routes, creating fortified bridges, caravan stops, smuggling corridors, and isolated settlements.
- Lake Aelorin and the main river provide the continent's most important natural east-west transport corridor.
