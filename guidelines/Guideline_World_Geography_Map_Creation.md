# Guideline: World Geography & Map Creation

**Purpose**: This document provides a checklist of what should be included when designing a fantasy world map or regional geography. It helps ensure maps are functional, immersive, and useful for both DM and players.

---

## 1. Basic Scale & Scope
- Decide the overall size of the continent or region (in miles or travel days).
- Choose map scale (e.g., 1 inch = 50 miles, 100 miles, etc.).
- Decide zoom level (world map, continent map, regional map, or city map).

## 2. Major Landforms
- **Mountains / Mountain Ranges** (with names and rough locations)
- **Hills / Rolling Hills**
- **Plains / Grasslands**
- **Forests** (dense, light, magical, ancient, etc.)
- **Deserts** or arid regions
- **Swamps / Wetlands**
- **Tundra / Frozen Highlands**
- **Coastlines** and **Islands**
- **Ravines, Canyons, or Major Cracks** in the earth (if any)

## 3. Water Features
- **Major Rivers** (with sources and paths)
- **Lakes** and **Inland Seas**
- **Deltas** and **Estuaries**
- **Coastal Features** (bays, fjords, cliffs)
- **Oceans / Seas** bordering the land

## 4. Climate & Weather
- General climate zones (temperate, tropical, arid, cold, etc.)
- Seasonal patterns (how weather changes throughout the year)
- How climate affects travel, agriculture, and settlements

## 5. Settlements
- **Major Cities** (with clear symbols and labels)
- **Towns and Villages** (especially along roads and rivers)
- **Trade Hubs / Crossroads**
- **Fortresses / Strongholds**
- **Ports and Harbors**

## 6. Infrastructure & Travel
- **Major Roads / Trade Routes** (clearly drawn)
- **Minor Roads / Paths**
- **Bridges** and **Fords**
- **Mountain Passes**
- **Dangerous Routes** (e.g., through dead zones or monster territory)

## 7. Points of Interest
- **Ruins** and **Ancient Sites**
- **Dungeons** or **Caves**
- **Magical or Cursed Locations** (e.g., dead zones — make them visually distinct)
- **Natural Wonders** (waterfalls, volcanoes, giant trees, etc.)
- **Landmarks** players can see from a distance

## 8. Political / Cultural Boundaries (Optional but Recommended)
- Borders between kingdoms, regions, or racial territories
- Areas of influence or contested land
- Dead zones or "blighted" regions (visually distinct — darker, cracked, faded)

## 9. Visual & Stylistic Elements
- **Compass Rose**
- **Scale Bar**
- **Legend / Key**
- Consistent iconography (mountains, trees, cities, roads, etc.)
- Color palette that matches the tone of your world
- Labels that are easy to read

## 10. Functional Considerations for Play
- Travel times between locations (on foot, horseback, ship)
- Chokepoints and strategic locations
- Areas that are hard to reach or dangerous
- How the geography affects trade, war, and daily life

---

**Best Practice Tips**:
- Start broad (continent shape + major features), then zoom in.
- Make dead zones or magical anomalies **visually distinct** so players immediately recognize them.
- Include smaller towns/villages along roads — they make the world feel lived-in.
- Roads should connect important places logically (people build roads where they travel most).

Use this checklist when creating new regions or expanding your map.
