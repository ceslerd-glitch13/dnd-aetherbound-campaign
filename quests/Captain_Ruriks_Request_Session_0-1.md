# Captain Rurik's Request — Session 0 / Session 1 Starter

**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Recommended starting level:** 1  
**Primary location:** The Rusty Anchor, The Wayward Gull, and Ravensport Docks
**Quest type:** Investigation with social, stealth, chase, and optional combat resolutions

## Quest Summary

Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik hires the party to recover a silver medallion stolen from the strongroom aboard his working ship, **The Wayward Gull**. Neither he nor the party initially knows that the medallion is only a protective housing for an unmarked Aetherite power orb intended for Silas Crowe and Lord Cassian Veyne.

The quest is designed to introduce Ravensport, normal gate-city life, the party's first recurring allies, and the campaign's central mystery without forcing the party to keep, return, or study the orb.

# Session 0 — The Rusty Anchor

## Session Goals

- Introduce the player characters and establish why each is in Ravensport.
- Present dimensional danger as a normal part of gate-city life.
- Establish Rurik as competent, blunt, fair, and worth trusting.
- Introduce the Rusty Anchor as a possible home base.
- Offer the first job and let the party choose its initial lead.

## The Rusty Anchor

The Rusty Anchor is a large two-story riverfront inn marked by a slightly crooked anchor above the main door. The common room smells of salt, ale, roasted meat, pipe smoke, wet rope, and old wood. Sailors, guards, traders, dockworkers, and adventurers share crowded tables.

### Staff

- **Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik:** Owner, semi-retired ship captain, and quest giver.
- **Mira “Red” Tanner:** Head bartender and cook; observant and difficult to intimidate.
- **Jorik Broadshoulder:** Dwarven bouncer and handyman; quiet and physically imposing.
- **Maelis “Silverthorn”:** Half-elven server and information broker with a concealed Voss family connection.

## Opening Moment — The Sound from the Heart

As the characters enter, settle in, or begin speaking with one another, a distant alarm sounds from Ravensport's Heart.

A few seconds later, something enormous roars or strikes the inner fortifications. Cups tremble. Conversation pauses. Rurik stops what he is doing and listens for the follow-up signal.

The containment signal sounds. The room resumes its activity.

Rurik may say:

> “Inner wall held. Finish your drinks before the next one decides to interrupt supper.”

Do not ask for fear checks. The moment demonstrates that gate danger is serious, familiar, and part of daily life.

## Rurik Before the Offer

Rurik is not waiting in a corner for adventurers. He is reviewing a damaged cargo manifest, speaking quietly with a crew member, and checking the key to the strongroom aboard **The Wayward Gull**, his working ship moored at South Pier.

Maelis notices that the characters appear capable and, more importantly, are not tied to Rurik's crew, the dock guilds, or the local criminal groups. She quietly suggests that outsiders may be useful.

## Rurik's Initial Test

Rurik asks direct questions that allow the players to introduce their characters:

> “Can you keep your mouths shut?”  
> “Can you find something without setting half the docks on fire?”  
> “And if the person who took it turns out to be wearing my colors, can you bring me proof before you start swinging?”

Their answers establish Rurik's first impression but should not prevent the quest from being offered.

## Rurik's Pitch

Read or paraphrase:

> “Three days ago I was hired to bring a small, ordinary-looking shipment from an outer port straight to Ravensport. A silver necklace in a locked box. The client paid half upfront and promised the rest on delivery.”
>
> “Last night, after we docked, someone opened my strongroom and took that box. No broken lock. No scattered valuables. Whoever did it knew where to look and what to take.”
>
> “The final client is important, impatient, and wealthy enough to make my life difficult. If I do not recover his property, I lose the payment and may lose the ship with it.”
>
> “Find who took it and bring the necklace back. Talk, sneak, threaten, or drag it out of the river—I do not care. But do not murder some fool over a necklace, do not start a war in my name, and do not lie to me about what you find.”

### Reward

- **50 gp upfront** for expenses.
- **150 gp when the medallion is recovered and returned to Rurik**, regardless of the later decision about the orb.
- One month of room and board at the Rusty Anchor after a good-faith completion.
- Future favors and a friendly relationship with Rurik.

### What Rurik Knows

- The client used the name **Silas Crowe**.
- Silas appeared wealthy, formal, and dangerous.
- The cargo was presented as an ordinary but expensive silver necklace.
- The shipment was supposed to be discreet.
- Rurik does not know the necklace conceals an orb or why Silas wants it.

### What Rurik Does Not Know

- The existence or mechanics of unmarked Aetherite power orbs.
- The orb's dead-zone field or charge capacity.
- Veyne's three-crystal transit array.
- Silas's full connection to Veyne.
- The relationship between dead zones, beacon cores, and Aetheron.

## Session 0 Investigation Start

The party may immediately:

- inspect the strongroom aboard **The Wayward Gull** at South Pier;
- question Rurik's crew;
- speak with Mira, Jorik, or Maelis;
- examine unloading records;
- walk the docks and question laborers;
- ask about fences or stolen cargo in the Shrouded Wards.

Session 0 should end after the party chooses its first meaningful lead. Do not force recovery of the medallion during the introductory session.

# Session 1 — The Missing Cargo

## Investigation Principle

The party has three independent clue routes. Failed checks change how quickly or cleanly information is obtained; they do not stop the quest.

## Route 1 — The Strongroom

**The Wayward Gull** is moored at South Pier near the Rusty Anchor. A narrow loading ramp, tired deckhands, and a newly replaced strongroom lock make the correct ship and evidence scene easy to identify.

The party can discover:

- The lock was opened rather than broken.
- Someone used an imperfect duplicate key.
- A trace of colored impression wax remains inside the mechanism.
- Coin, documents, and other valuables were ignored.
- The padded case was removed cleanly.

Suggested checks:

- **DC 10 Investigation:** The thief knew exactly which box to take.
- **DC 12 Investigation or thieves' tools:** The key was duplicated recently and imperfectly.
- **DC 14 Investigation:** The impression was probably taken during a brief opportunity rather than by someone with permanent access.

On failure, Rurik or Jorik can still point out that nothing else was taken.

## Route 2 — Witnesses at the Docks

Possible information:

- Mira remembers a temporary dockworker asking when Rurik's ship would finish unloading.
- Jorik saw a covered handcart moving toward the Shrouded Wards after midnight.
- Maelis noticed someone asking about Rurik's “special client.”
- A sailor remembers an unfamiliar worker wearing a legitimate-looking but outdated dock badge.
- A laborer saw the worker speak with a person wearing a gray scarf.

Suggested checks:

- **DC 10 Persuasion:** Obtain basic witness descriptions.
- **DC 12 Insight:** Identify a witness who is afraid rather than dishonest.
- **DC 13 Investigation:** Connect the outdated badge to a copied guild mark.

Bribes, kindness, intimidation, or favors can replace a roll when appropriate.

Witnesses can also direct the party to **Netmaker's Bunkhouse**, a crowded temporary-worker boarding house on the Docks/Shrouded Wards boundary where Orren rented a room. His debts, outdated badge, and neighbors who saw the gray-scarved runner provide recoverable leads.

## Route 3 — Records and Street Leads

The unloading roster contains:

- one temporary worker added in different ink;
- a false address;
- a copied guild mark;
- a warehouse token tied to an abandoned sailmaker's building.

Corvin Rook at Last Chance Curios can identify the token or direct the party toward a minor runner connected to Nyra Vell's network.

Suggested checks:

- **DC 11 Investigation:** Notice the different ink and false address.
- **DC 13 forgery kit, Investigation, or relevant background:** Identify the copied guild mark.
- **DC 12 Persuasion or payment to Corvin:** Learn where the token is used.

## The Immediate Thief — Orren Pike

Orren Pike is a struggling dockworker who was paid to copy Rurik's key and steal one particular box. He believed it contained jewelry or ordinary contraband.

He knows:

- the runner who hired him used a gray scarf and a false name;
- the exact sailmaker's loft where he delivered the box;
- he was paid through a coded Shrouded Wards token;
- the smugglers knew the ship, strongroom, unloading schedule, and exact box;
- he knows nothing about Aetherite technology, Veyne, or Aetheron.

Orren is most easily found through **Netmaker's Bunkhouse**, though he may already be hiding, moving between dockside contacts, or attempting to flee. He may confess, bargain for protection, or lead the party to the recovery location. He is not a fanatical enemy and should not fight to the death.

## Recovery Location — Old Gull Sailworks

The medallion is held in a concealed upper loft while smugglers arrange a buyer. The building stands beside a narrow Shrouded Wards canal, roughly inland from East Pier. A faded white gull remains painted over the front doors, hanging sailcloth conceals the loft access, and a canal-side loading entrance provides a second way inside.

The party may:

- enter through the front under a false identity;
- climb into the upper loft;
- use a canal-side loading entrance;
- negotiate through Corvin Rook;
- follow Orren or a runner;
- create a distraction;
- fight the guards.

### Possible Opposition

Use `../combat/Enemy_Encounters_Stat_Blocks.md` as appropriate:

- Dockside Lookouts;
- Smugglers;
- one Smuggler Enforcer for a dangerous level-1 confrontation;
- city guards as a legal complication if the scene becomes loud.

Nyra Vell is not personally waiting in the loft. Her connection appears through a payment token, coded instruction, or subordinate.

### Evidence at the Loft

At least one clue survives regardless of the method used:

- a coded payment chit;
- a buyer inquiry mentioning “blue glass” or “power storage”;
- a route mark leading west;
- a false cargo seal;
- a note indicating that someone connected to Silas's operation leaked the shipment details.

## Returning the Medallion

When the party returns, Rurik pays the remaining reward and hands the medallion to Maelis for discreet storage until Silas collects it.

If the party has already exposed the orb, Maelis sees it immediately. If the housing remains closed, damage or loosening from the theft allows her to notice a concealed seam and open it.

### Maelis's Recognition

Maelis recognizes the object as an **unmarked Aetherite power orb** because she once saw one during a private interaction involving her Voss family connections and has heard restricted rumors about blue-crystal technology.

She knows:

- it stores magical power;
- it is rare and treated seriously by high-ranking officials;
- it is not ordinary jewelry;
- transporting it concealed inside a necklace suggests an attempt to avoid official attention.

She does not know:

- the exact 24-charge rules;
- the full dead-zone field mechanics;
- Veyne's transit array;
- why Silas specifically needs this orb.

### Private Conversation

Maelis pulls Rurik aside. Anyone nearby automatically notices that the exchange is serious.

A character close enough may attempt a **DC 15 Wisdom (Perception)** check to hear or lip-read the important words. Clever positioning, an appropriate feature, or a clear method may remove the need for the roll.

> **Maelis:** “Do not contact Crowe.”  
> **Rurik:** “We recovered his property. That was the agreement.”  
> **Maelis:** “He told you this was jewelry. It is not.”  
> **Rurik:** “Then what is it?”  
> **Maelis:** “An unmarked Aetherite power orb. I have seen one before.”  
> **Rurik:** “Where?”  
> **Maelis:** “Somewhere you were never meant to ask about. Objects like this do not travel hidden inside necklaces unless someone is avoiding official attention.”  
> **Rurik:** “Do you know what it does?”  
> **Maelis:** “Not exactly. I know it stores power, I know people with authority treat them seriously, and I know Crowe lied to you. Tell him it was never recovered.”  
> **Rurik:** “And what do we do with it?”  
> **Maelis:** “We do nothing with it. It does not stay in this tavern, it does not go aboard your ship, and it does not pass through my hands again.”

## The Party's Decision

### Take or Study the Orb

Rurik does not demand a high Persuasion check. Maelis has already convinced him that keeping the object at the tavern or aboard his ship is dangerous.

Rurik says:

> “You recovered the necklace and earned your payment. What was hidden inside it was never mentioned in the contract. If you are foolish enough to take responsibility for it, take it—and as far as Crowe is concerned, the whole thing stayed stolen.”

The party gains the orb. Rurik may ask that it be kept away from his crew and tavern. Veyne's Organization Interest rises according to the tracker once Silas suspects the truth.

### Return the Orb to Silas Anyway

The party may persuade Rurik to complete the delivery despite Maelis's warning.

- **DC 16 Charisma (Persuasion):** Basic argument that the property belongs to the client or that honoring the contract is safer.
- **DC 13:** The party offers to attend the exchange, protect Rurik, document the handoff, or accept responsibility for consequences.
- No roll is required when the group presents overwhelming practical evidence or a plan Rurik considers clearly safer.

On success:

- Rurik contacts Silas and completes the delivery.
- The party receives its full reward.
- Maelis remains cautious but does not create a public confrontation.
- Silas regards the party as competent and worth observing.
- Veyne's organization gains the battery and its operational timeline advances.
- The party loses immediate access to the orb but retains every open-world route into the main story.

### Give the Orb to Ravensport Authorities

The party may approach Thorne Blackwood, Lirael Starveil, Seraphine Vale, or another official.

The orb enters official jurisdiction, but the party may remain involved as witnesses, investigators, or temporary field custodians. Silas avoids a public confrontation and begins operating more carefully.

### Hide, Abandon, or Destroy the Housing

The orb can be hidden separately from the medallion. Hiding it creates a retrieval race. Abandoning it allows another faction to find it later. Destroying the silver housing does not destroy the orb.

### Refuse Further Involvement

Rurik and Maelis remove the item from the tavern as quickly as possible. The main story remains available through the five open-world quests in `Early_Sessions_Plan_Sessions_0_to_5.md` and `Quests_Player_Decisions_Impacts.md`.

## Milestone

Award level 2 after the party recovers the medallion and resolves the return scene, regardless of which faction receives the orb.

# Resonance Orb Reference — DM Only

- **Housing:** Silver medallion; the housing is not the power source.
- **Orb:** Smooth unmarked Aetherite power orb.
- **Attunement:** None.
- **Maximum charges:** 24.
- **Recharge:** 1 charge per hour in usable ambient magic.
- **Dead-zone field:** 5-foot radius centered on the orb; 1 charge per hour for the entire field.
- **Spell cost:** 8 charges per spell level, up to 3rd level.
- **Cantrips:** No charge cost; cannot be upcast.
- **Anti-magic:** Does not automatically override a deliberate anti-magic field.
- **Hidden purpose:** One battery component in Veyne's three-crystal Aetheron transit array.

Track the orb's holder and charges in `Quests_Player_Decisions_Impacts.md`.
