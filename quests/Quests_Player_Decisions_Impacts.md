# Quests, Player Decisions & Impacts Tracker

## Purpose

This is the living record of quests, player decisions, faction reactions, NPC attitudes, the Resonance Orb, and the attention of Veyne's organization.

## Status Vocabulary

- **Not Started:** Offered or established, but no meaningful action has occurred.
- **Potential:** Discoverable in the world, but not yet accepted.
- **Locked:** Cannot begin until a stated trigger or decision occurs.
- **In Progress:** The party has taken meaningful action toward the objective.
- **Completed:** The objective was resolved.
- **Failed / Transformed:** The original objective can no longer be completed, but its consequences continue as a changed quest.

# Active and Potential Quests

## Captain Rurik's Request

- **Status:** Not Started
- **Quest giver:** Captain Elandor "Blackscale" Rurik
- **Location:** The Rusty Anchor and The Wayward Gull at South Pier, Ravensport Docks
- **Primary scene anchors:** The Wayward Gull -> Netmaker's Bunkhouse -> Old Gull Sailworks.
- **Objective:** Recover the stolen silver medallion and the unmarked Aetherite power orb concealed inside it.
- **Known involved NPCs:** Rurik; Maelis "Silverthorn"; Silas Crowe; Orren Pike; Corvin Rook; possible smugglers connected to Nyra Vell.
- **Reward:** 50 gp upfront, 150 gp on recovery, one month of room and board, and future favors from Rurik.
- **Starting leads:** Search the strongroom; question tavern staff, sailors, or dockworkers; inspect unloading records; follow a warehouse token toward the Shrouded Wards.
- **Hidden consequence:** Returning the orb advances Veyne's ability to prepare or complete another crossing to Aetheron.
- **Combat possibilities:** Lookouts, smugglers, one enforcer, guard intervention, or a chase. Combat is not required.

# Veyne's Organization Interest

Track how much attention Veyne's organization gives the party, separately from whether Veyne's larger plans are succeeding.

## Current Interest

- **Starting level:** 0 — Unaware
- **Expected change during Rurik's Request:** The level normally becomes 1 once Silas learns the party is actively investigating.

| Level | State | Typical response |
|---:|---|---|
| 0 | Unaware | The organization does not know or care who the party is. |
| 1 | Noted | Names and descriptions are recorded; low-level observers may watch. |
| 2 | Watched | Rooms, contacts, purchases, and travel are quietly monitored. |
| 3 | Tested | Bribes, false jobs, staged threats, theft, or controlled confrontation. |
| 4 | Active Retrieval | Operatives attempt to recover evidence, technology, or witnesses. |
| 5 | Elimination Authorized | The party knows enough that capture, disappearance, or death is ordered. |

## Suggested Interest Triggers

Increase interest by one when the party:

- keeps the orb after Silas expects delivery;
- publicly asks about Aetherite power systems;
- exposes or disrupts a Veyne operation;
- captures or kills an operative;
- enters or removes evidence from a beacon site;
- identifies Veyne as the patron behind Silas;
- learns the connection between Aetheron, the transit array, and harvested magic.

Do not automatically increase interest for every minor success. Use the highest meaningful trigger from a scene.

## Interest Modifiers

- **Return the orb:** Interest normally remains at 1. Silas views the party as competent prospects rather than immediate enemies, while Veyne's operational timeline advances.
- **Keep the orb quietly:** Interest becomes at least 2.
- **Give the orb to officials:** Interest becomes at least 2 and may become 3 if official custody threatens exposure.
- **Destroy an operation but leave no identifying evidence:** Interest may rise more slowly.
- **Accept work from Silas:** Interest does not necessarily decrease; the party may instead be evaluated for recruitment or manipulation.
- **Reach level 5 interest:** Operatives generally stop using ordinary intimidation and attempt to silence the party.

# NPC Relationships and Baseline Attitudes

## Captain Elandor "Blackscale" Rurik

- **Starting attitude:** Neutral, assessing.
- **Helpful behavior:** Honest effort, protection of crew and staff, respect for his ship and tavern.
- **Negative behavior:** Lying after accepting payment, endangering civilians, stealing from him, or bringing Silas's retaliation to his crew without warning.
- **Likely progression:** Friendly after a good-faith recovery attempt. Wary if the party keeps the orb dishonestly. Still friendly if he knowingly agrees that they should take it away.

## Maelis "Silverthorn"

- **Starting attitude:** Cautiously helpful and observant.
- **Knowledge:** Recognizes an unmarked Aetherite power orb from restricted rumors and one prior encounter through her high-standing Voss family connections.
- **Helpful behavior:** Discretion, protecting Rurik, asking careful questions, and taking the danger seriously.
- **Negative behavior:** Publicly exposing her family connection, recklessly displaying the orb, or informing Silas of her warning.
- **Likely progression:** Becomes a recurring intelligence contact if trusted.

## Silas Crowe

- **Starting attitude:** Unknown to the party; privately indifferent.
- **After party involvement:** Assessing at interest level 1.
- **Helpful behavior:** Returning the orb, demonstrating discretion, accepting controlled work, or proving useful.
- **Negative behavior:** Withholding the orb, exposing his role, following him, or harming his operatives.
- **Likely progression:** Polite patron, manipulator, or targeted enemy depending on utility and knowledge.

## Nyra "the Thread" Vell

- **Starting attitude:** Unknown and pragmatic.
- **Helpful behavior:** Respecting negotiated boundaries, offering profitable information, or avoiding unnecessary attacks on her network.
- **Negative behavior:** Public humiliation, indiscriminate killing of smugglers, theft without negotiation, or turning over complete ledgers.
- **Likely progression:** Rival, information source, temporary ally, or enemy. She does not serve Veyne loyally.

## Captain Thorne Blackwood

- **Starting attitude:** Neutral and professional.
- **Helpful behavior:** Providing evidence, limiting civilian harm, respecting warrants, and distinguishing ordinary crime from existential threats.
- **Negative behavior:** Attacking guards, destroying evidence, or using the Heart as cover for personal crime.
- **Likely progression:** Trusted official contact if the party proves reliable.

## Archmage Lirael Starveil

- **Starting attitude:** Neutral and intellectually interested.
- **Helpful behavior:** Honest disclosure of dangerous technology, careful testing, and willingness to accept safety restrictions.
- **Negative behavior:** Reckless experimentation, concealing an active threat, or attempting to sell Aetherite technology.
- **Likely progression:** Technical adviser or custodian dispute depending on the orb decision.

## Brogan Ashdelve

- **Starting attitude:** Neutral and practical.
- **Helpful behavior:** Competence, protecting supplies, respecting workers, and solving problems without wasting resources.
- **Negative behavior:** Treating Deepanvil as primitive, risking caravans for curiosity, or relying on magic without a backup plan.
- **Likely progression:** Reliable western-road and Deepanvil contact.

## Lord Cassian Veyne

- **Starting attitude:** Unaware.
- **When informed:** Evaluates the party as assets, obstacles, or witnesses.
- **Likely progression:** Recruitment or misdirection at low interest; retrieval and elimination at high interest.
- **Important:** An early fight is possible but not balanced for a starter party. Record any meeting immediately because it radically changes antagonist knowledge and planning.
