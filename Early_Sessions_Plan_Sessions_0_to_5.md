# Early Campaign Plan — Sessions 0 to 5

**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Target levels:** 1–4 during the opening stage  
**Primary base:** Ravensport  
**Core philosophy:** Open-world play with a strong opening hook, multiple independent quests, and no required sequence after Captain Rurik's Request

## How to Use This File

- Session 0 and the core of Session 1 are prepared in detail through `Captain_Ruriks_Request_Session_0-1.md`.
- After the medallion is recovered—or ignored—the party may choose any available quest.
- Do not pre-script several sessions for an unchosen branch.
- Before each later session, prepare only the quest, locations, NPCs, and encounters the players have actually selected.
- Every quest stands on its own while containing at least one recoverable connection to Veyne's organization, Aetherite technology, dead zones, or beacon harvesting.
- Failure changes the world or moves evidence; it does not end the campaign.

---

# Session 0 — The Rusty Anchor

## Required Beats

1. Give every player a brief reason to be at the Rusty Anchor.
2. Use a contained Heart incident to demonstrate that gate danger is a normal part of Ravensport life.
3. Introduce Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik as the party's first major NPC.
4. Let Rurik test the party through direct questions rather than a formal interview.
5. Offer the job to recover the stolen medallion.
6. Introduce Mira “Red” Tanner, Jorik Broadshoulder, and Maelis “Silverthorn.”
7. Let the players choose their first investigative lead.

Session 0 should end with a decision and momentum, not with a forced recovery or mandatory item choice.

Full scene text, Rurik's pitch, tavern details, and initial clues are in `Captain_Ruriks_Request_Session_0-1.md`.

# Session 1 — Captain Rurik's Request

## Core Objective

Recover the stolen silver medallion from the Shrouded Wards and decide what to do after Maelis recognizes the unmarked Aetherite power orb hidden inside it.

## Investigation Routes

- Strongroom evidence: duplicate key, impression wax, targeted theft.
- Witnesses: temporary dockworker, covered handcart, outdated badge, gray-scarved runner.
- Records and street contacts: altered roster, false address, copied guild mark, warehouse token, Corvin Rook.

All routes point toward Orren Pike and an abandoned sailmaker's loft. Failed checks change time, cost, attention, or complications rather than blocking progress.

## Possible Conflict

Use only what fits the party's approach:

- Dockside Lookouts;
- Smugglers;
- one Smuggler Enforcer;
- a chase with Orren Pike;
- city guards arriving after public violence;
- burning records or collapsing access as noncombat complications.

The appropriate statistics are in `Enemy_Encounters_Stat_Blocks.md`.

## Return Decision

Maelis recognizes the orb when Rurik hands the recovered medallion to her for storage. She recommends that Rurik tell Silas it was never recovered. The party may keep it, return it, give it to officials, hide it, abandon it, or refuse further involvement.

The exact scene and consequences are in `Captain_Ruriks_Request_Session_0-1.md`.

## Milestone

The party reaches level 2 after recovering the medallion and resolving the return scene, regardless of who receives the orb.

# Open-World Quest Board

These five quests can exist at the same time. They may appear as job postings, rumors, private requests, official inquiries, or consequences of earlier choices.

## 1. The Vanished Supply Caravan

- **Discovery:** Mara Reed, Brogan Ashdelve, a job posting, or rumors of overdue Deepanvil supplies.
- **Objective:** Locate the missing caravan, rescue survivors, and recover food, medicine, lift parts, precision tools, and rescue equipment.
- **Visible stakes:** Deepanvil shortages and endangered workers.
- **Complication:** False road markers diverted the caravan so selected mechanical components could be removed.
- **Main-story connection:** The stolen components are useful in a small beacon installation; payment and shipping marks connect indirectly to Silas.
- **Possible opposition:** Route Thieves, a Silent Hand Operative, or a Dead-Zone Stalker.
- **Possible resolutions:** Rescue, recovery, negotiation, pursuit, exposure of false records, or following the diverted parts.
- **Reward:** Payment, Deepanvil goodwill, engineering access, and a western contact.
- **Failure:** Deepanvil's shortages worsen and Veyne receives the components.

## 2. The Blue Lantern Auction

- **Discovery:** Corvin Rook, Maelis, a criminal invitation, or observation of unusual buyers.
- **Objective:** Infiltrate, observe, expose, rob, or manipulate a private Shrouded Wards auction.
- **Visible stakes:** Stolen cargo, dangerous planar specimens, and restricted devices entering private hands.
- **Complication:** A concealed Veyne buyer wants damaged Aetherite technology.
- **Main-story connection:** Buyer records and courier routes reveal additional purchases and western transfers.
- **Possible opposition:** Lookouts, Smugglers, an Enforcer, a Retrieval Specialist, or Silas Crowe.
- **Possible resolutions:** Pose as buyers, steal the ledger, trigger a guard raid, bargain with Nyra Vell, replace a lot, or follow the winner.
- **Reward:** Criminal intelligence, recovered goods, money, or a favor from officials or Nyra.
- **Failure:** The device reaches Veyne, but witnesses and payment trails remain.

## 3. The Third Alarm

- **Discovery:** Three unusual Heart incidents, a discreet request from Thorne Blackwood, or a warning from Maelis.
- **Objective:** Determine why separate gate incursions display nearly identical behavior and timing.
- **Visible stakes:** Routine incursions are expected; coordinated testing is not.
- **Complication:** Each creature carries a manufactured physical marker recording defensive response times.
- **Main-story connection:** Someone is measuring anti-magic activation, troop movement, and containment procedures.
- **Possible opposition:** A Marked Gate Beast or Silent Hand observer.
- **Possible resolutions:** Analyze the marker, interview defenders, trace the material, inspect emergence points, or identify a buyer of security information.
- **Reward:** Official trust, limited Heart access, and a favor from security leadership.
- **Failure:** The hostile observer gains better knowledge of Ravensport's defenses.

## 4. The Silent Orchard

- **Discovery:** Maera Thornwhisper, a Faerindel trade envoy, healers, or interrupted medicine shipments.
- **Objective:** Investigate geometric zones of magical depletion in a medicinal grove.
- **Visible stakes:** Regional medicine production is declining.
- **Complication:** Buried conduits are drawing magical energy away from the grove.
- **Main-story connection:** The conduits are an experimental form of beacon-harvesting technology built with mixed elven, dwarven, and human expertise.
- **Possible opposition:** A Conduit Warden or Silent Hand Operative.
- **Possible resolutions:** Remove or follow the conduits, preserve them for study, expose a contractor, or leave them operating as bait.
- **Reward:** Medicine, healing assistance, Faerindel goodwill, and specialist knowledge.
- **Failure:** Medicine shortages worsen and the harvesting network expands.

## 5. The Unclaimed Workshop

- **Discovery:** A landlord, Ironforge representatives, a mechanical accident, or a missing-person report.
- **Objective:** Find a missing craftsperson and secure dangerous unfinished machinery.
- **Visible stakes:** The workshop contains proprietary designs and unstable equipment near civilian housing.
- **Complication:** The engineer was hired to connect anti-magic-compatible mechanics to an unfamiliar blue-crystal power interface.
- **Main-story connection:** The design is a portable beacon regulator or Aetherite-powered weapon platform.
- **Possible opposition:** Workshop Defense Automata, an Aetherite Survey Construct, or a Retrieval Specialist.
- **Possible resolutions:** Rescue the engineer, dismantle the prototype, finish it as bait, turn it over to Ironforge, sell it, or follow the retrieval team.
- **Reward:** Payment, engineering credit, prototype access, or Ironforge goodwill.
- **Failure:** Veyne recovers the prototype and the missing engineer remains endangered.

# Main-Story Convergence

No quest must reveal the full campaign truth. Each should provide one or more of the following:

- Aetherite technology is being acquired through concealed networks.
- Components are being moved toward hidden installations.
- Magical energy is being harvested rather than generated.
- Silas serves a better-funded patron.
- Veyne's personnel destroy evidence and silence knowledgeable witnesses.
- Similar operations may exist elsewhere.

Do not reveal Veyne's complete plan, the full three-crystal transit system, or the entire truth of Aetheron unless play naturally reaches that information.

---

# Early Encounter with Veyne

Open-world choices may lead the party to Lord Cassian Veyne far earlier than expected.

- Veyne may negotiate, recruit, mislead, threaten, or order subordinates to act.
- Once the party knows enough to threaten an operation, his trained personnel generally attempt recovery, capture, disappearance, or elimination.
- Veyne is not scaled down merely because the party finds him early.
- Use his contingency entry in `Enemy_Encounters_Stat_Blocks.md`.
- Provide believable opportunities for retreat, surrender, deception, distraction, or environmental escape.
- If the party defeats him through extraordinary planning, alliances, luck, or dead-zone exploitation, accept the result and restructure the campaign around the power vacuum.

# Resonance and Dead-Zone Reference

## Resonance Orb

- 24 maximum charges.
- Recharges 1 charge per hour in usable ambient magic.
- Creates a 5-foot dead-zone protection field for 1 charge per hour.
- Spells cost 8 charges per spell level, maximum 3rd level.
- Cantrips cost no charges.
- Does not automatically defeat a deliberate anti-magic field.

## Beacon-Core Field

Within 20 feet of an active beacon core:

- casting is possible in the surrounding dead zone;
- each cast requires a Constitution check, DC 10 + original spell level; cantrips use DC 10;
- failure creates a DM-selected unintended result and uses normal slot cost;
- success halves slot cost, rounded up; 1st-level spells cost no slot;
- spells keep their original level for other rules;
- duration doubles while the relevant effect remains inside the field;
- damaging and healing spells add 1d6 once per affected creature per casting.

The World Bible remains the authority for complete mechanics.
