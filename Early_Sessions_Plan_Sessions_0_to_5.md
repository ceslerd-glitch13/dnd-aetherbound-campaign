# Early Campaign Plan: Sessions 0–5 (Focused Starter Arc)

**Campaign:** Untitled D&D Campaign  
**Target Player Level:** 1–4 (by end of Session 5)  
**Primary Location:** Ravensport and immediate surroundings (Docks, Grand Bazaar, Shrouded Wards, Gate Quarter). One optional path leads west toward Deepanvil.  
**Core Philosophy:** Sandbox-style play with a strong central hook (the Resonance Medallion theft). Multiple branching paths that players can pursue in any order or combination. All paths seed the larger Veyne's organization / dead zone / Aetheron plot without railroading. Players may never leave Ravensport if they choose urban-focused play.  
**Design Notes**: 
- Intended to use the existing guidelines. Several mandatory quest, NPC, encounter, and cross-reference elements remain incomplete and must be verified before play.
- Planned encounters may use official D&D references as baselines, but this file does not yet contain complete encounter templates or campaign-ready stat-block references.
- Map and travel authority: use `World_Geography.md` and the Ravensport profile. This file does not yet contain a complete starter-area location guide.
- NPCs: some key early NPCs have fuller entries in the master NPC database, but the NPC section below is not complete.
- Branching: The medallion recovery is the inciting incident. Player choices (keep it, turn it in, ignore side hooks, pursue rumors) determine path. DM can weave paths together (e.g., smuggling ring involved in medallion theft; mining delivery uncovers related sabotage).
- **Beacon Core Rule (Approved Canon; High-Level Secret):** A hidden beacon core creates a 20-foot supercharged resonance field while harvesting magic for Aetheron. Every spellcast requires a Constitution check (DC 10 + original spell level; cantrips DC 10). Failure causes wild magic or another DM-chosen unintended result and does not reduce the slot cost. Success halves the slot level, rounded up; 1st-level spells cost no slot. Spells retain their original level for all other rules, gain double duration while maintained in the field, and revert by halving remaining duration when the effect leaves. Each creature damaged or healed gains one additional 1d6 force damage or healing once per casting.

## Session 0: Character Creation + The Rusty Anchor Hook

**Goals**:
- Character introductions, backstories tied to Ravensport/gates/dead zones/trade.
- Establish core world elements: Dimensional gates, magic dead zones (one ~2 hours walk west of city), racial/cultural dynamics, Aetherite technology as rare/expensive, beacon absorption mechanics.
- Deliver first job: Recover the stolen Resonance Medallion for Captain Rurik.
- Plant seeds for all branching paths via rumors, staff interactions, and the item itself.
- End with clear next steps and player agency.

**Structure Outline**:
1. **Arrival at The Rusty Anchor (Docks District)**: Lively tavern. Describe smells, sounds, crowd.
2. **Meet Captain Elandor “Blackscale” Rurik**: Grizzled human with eyepatch. He gives the pitch.
3. **Initial Roleplay & Rumors**: Players can talk to staff or patrons. Lirael "Silverthorn" drops hints about smuggling, western troubles, or odd mechanical issues.
4. **The Theft Details**: Box stolen from strongroom. Client is "very important" (linked to Veyne's organization).
5. **Reward & Hook**: 50 gp upfront, 150 gp on return + room/board + favors. Medallion description given (ordinary silver necklace with hidden blue crystal).
6. **Investigation Start**: Search docks, interview staff, or head to Bazaar / Shrouded Wards.
7. **End of Session 0**: Decision point with the medallion.

**Key Decision Point**: Keep/study it (dangerous path) or turn it in.

## Branching Quest Paths (Sessions 1–5)

**Path 1: The Resonance Medallion – Keep or Study It**  
**Development**: Identification or dead-zone testing reveals that the necklace is only a housing for a 24-charge unmarked Aetherite orb. The orb creates a 5-foot protective field, spends 1 charge per hour inside a dead zone, and can supply 8 charges per spell level up to 3rd level; cantrips are free. This draws attention from Veyne's organization and may lead toward beacon-core clues.

**Path 2: Turn In the Medallion for Reward**  
**Development**: The party unknowingly delivers one of the unmarked battery orbs required for Veyne's three-crystal Aetheron travel array. Client complications or later discoveries can reveal the significance of that choice.

**Path 3: Delivery Job to Deepanvil (Western Path)**  
**Development**: Caravan delivery of non-magical dwarven engineering equipment. Mine subquests (cave-ins, sabotage, mechanical anomalies near beacon core). Cargo: Reinforced mechanical drills, precision tools, vibration-dampening vessels (no anti-magic tools unless sabotage plot).

**Path 4: Investigate the Smuggling Ring in Shrouded Wards**  
**Development**: Urban intrigue. Ring moving Aetherite-related items or gate contraband. Ties to medallion or beacon tech.

**Convergence**: All paths can lead to beacon-core discoveries, Veyne's unnamed organization, and the connection between harvested magic and Aetheron.

## Approved Item and Transport Notes

- The Resonance Medallion requires no attunement; the orb's field is centered on the physical orb and can protect the holder plus adjacent creatures.
- A normal dead zone blocks magical transport when a required endpoint is unprotected.
- A resonance orb can support an endpoint inside its 5-foot field, but it does not automatically defeat a deliberate anti-magic field.
- Ongoing magic powered by the orb is suppressed after the relevant caster, target, or effect leaves the field inside a dead zone.
- Time spent protected by the orb does not create retroactive Magical Exhaustion.
